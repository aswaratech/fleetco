- **Date:** 2026-06-18
- **Decider:** Product owner (CEO)

## Acceptance

## Context

FleetCo records what the fleet **costs** but cannot **bill** a customer. Every money-bearing surface shipped to date is **cost-side**: `FuelLog` (`totalCostPaisa`), `ExpenseLog` (`amountPaisa`), and the per-vehicle cost / fuel-efficiency reports that aggregate them. There is no **revenue** anywhere in the system — no price on a `Trip`, no bill for a `Job`, no invoice for a `Customer`. The operator runs jobs for customers and gets paid, but that entire half of the business lives in a spreadsheet (or on paper), exactly the spreadsheet-replacement the project exists to retire. This ADR proposes FleetCo's **first revenue-side aggregate**: an `Invoice` that turns the customer/job/trip chain into a compliant Nepal VAT invoice.

The pieces the invoice is built from already exist:

- **The `Trip` is the billable unit (ADR-0003).** ADR-0003 fixed the Trip as the central aggregate precisely on the billing argument: "Customer revenue is invoiced from Trips on Jobs," and "A Customer's bill is the sum of the trip-prices for trips on their job." A `Job` groups Trips for a Customer (`Job.customerId` FK, ADR-0003: "a Job is a contract, and the Trips are the executions of that contract"). So the data spine for an invoice — Customer → Job → Trips — is already modelled and navigable.

  > **Correction (2026-07-02):** this is inaccurate. The Customer→Job half exists (`Job.customerId`), but the Job→Trip half does **not** — `Trip` has no `jobId` FK and `Job` has no `trips` back-relation (verified in `apps/api/prisma/schema.prisma`). An invoice therefore cannot be built by server-side-traversing a job's trips: `InvoicesService.buildFromJob` bills operator-supplied `tripId`s, and trip-level customer-ownership cannot be enforced. Adding `Trip.jobId` touches the central Trip aggregate (ADR-0003) and needs its own ADR amendment; tracked in `docs/tech-debt.md` → "`Trip` is not linked to `Job`…". This note is append-only — the claim above is left as-written per the ADR discipline.

- **The `Customer` carries the tax identity.** `Customer.panNumber` (`@unique` when present; the schema comment names it "Nepal PAN — the tax ID issued by the Inland Revenue Department") is the buyer's VAT/PAN registration — the field that makes a customer invoiceable on a VAT invoice. `Job.jobNumber` (`JOB-YYYY-NNNNN`) is the work reference an invoice cites.
- **The money discipline is settled.** CLAUDE.md's money-as-integer-paisa rule (1 NPR = 100 paisa, never floats) governs every amount, and the **half-up rounding precedent is concrete**: `FuelLogsService.deriveTotalCostPaisa` computes `Math.round((litersMl * pricePerLiterPaisa) / 1000)` — integer in, integer out, half-up at the paisa boundary, with `apps/web/src/lib/money.ts` `formatNpr` / `rupeesToPaisa` as the display/entry edge. Tax math (VAT, TDS) is the same discipline applied to a new computation: computed in integer paisa, half-up, never a float.
- **There is a sequential-numbering precedent to harden, not reuse.** `JobsService.nextJobNumber` generates `JOB-YYYY-NNNNN` server-side (current UTC year + highest-existing-per-year sequence + 1, retried up to 3× on `P2002`). It is the closest precedent — but it is **not gapless or tamper-proof**: deleting a job leaves a hole (or, if the deleted row was the max, *reuses* a number), the find-highest+1 scheme has a race the `@unique` index only backstops, and the number is keyed by the **Gregorian** UTC year. An invoice number must be **gapless within the Nepali fiscal year, never reused, and immutable once issued** (the IRD anti-tamper expectation). So invoice numbering **hardens** this precedent; it does not copy it (see commitment 4).
- **There is a cross-aggregate read/aggregation precedent.** `ReportsService` assembles per-vehicle figures from Trips / FuelLogs / ExpenseLogs via Prisma `groupBy` merged in memory. Assembling an invoice's billable lines from a Job's Trips is the same kind of cross-aggregate read, done through the owning services' public interfaces.
- **There is a dedicated-aggregate CRUD convention.** Every aggregate (`customers/`, `geofences/`, the proposed `ServiceSchedule`/`ServiceRecord` of ADR-0037) follows the ADR-0006 vertical slice: `model` + migration → service → controller (`AuthGuard`, Zod `.strict()`, the `{ items, total, skip, take, sortBy, sortDir }` list shape, `createdById` from the session, the standardized `P2002` → 409 / `P2003` → 400|409 / `P2025` → 404 error mapping) → tests against real Postgres → web pages + a nav button.
- **The BS calendar is shipped (ADR-0031 / ADR-0032).** `formatNepaliDate` renders any ISO date in Bikram Sambat, and the glossary records that "the Nepali fiscal year runs roughly from mid-July to mid-July (Shrawan to Ashadh)." Invoices are **dated and numbered by the Nepali fiscal year**, not the Gregorian one — a Nepal-specific compliance point this aggregate must get right (commitment 4) and render through the existing BS pipeline.
- **Object storage and data classification are settled.** Cloudflare R2 is the stack's object store (CLAUDE.md); ADR-0013 commits R2 default AES-256 encryption at rest for object storage; ADR-0014 commitment 6 made the first concrete use of R2 (encrypted offsite backups) and **explicitly deferred in-app R2 uploads to Phase 2**. A generated invoice PDF stored in R2 would be among the **first in-app R2 uses**. ADR-0013 named this exact data category in its Context: "In Phase 4 the system adds financial records that interact with Nepal's Inland Revenue Department."

### Phase mismatch and the compliance posture (stated honestly)

A fresh reader must see four facts plainly before the PO weighs the pull-forward:

1. **This proposes Phase-4 work two phases ahead of its phase — the furthest pull-forward yet.** Customer invoicing is **Phase-4 "Money" headline material**: the roadmap's §"Phase 4 — Money" names it directly — "close the loop on cash… customer invoicing, a basic chart of accounts, exports in IRD-friendly formats." The active phase is **Phase 2** (driver app + telematics), itself opened ahead of the Phase-1 daily-use gate. So this ADR proposes reaching **two phases past** the one in flight. It is proposed under the **ADR-0025 ahead-of-gate pattern** — the documented-exception mechanism that opened Phase 2 before Phase 1 reached daily use — but ADR-0025 was an explicit **one-time** exception scoped to the Phase-1 → Phase-2 transition only, and its "Revisit when" states plainly that "any further override needs its own ADR." Pulling Phase-4 scope forward is a **new, separately-decided exception**, exactly as ADR-0037 and ADR-0038 framed their own Phase-3 pull-forwards — and it is a larger jump than either of theirs. This ADR records the proposal; **accepting it is what authorizes the pull-forward.** An agent must never self-accept this ADR, add an `## Acceptance` block, or flip the status — the PO ratifies separately.

2. **The first production deploy is still owed.** Phase 1 is content-complete but **not in daily use** — the deploy, the restore-from-backup drill, live SLI/DORA reporting, and the retrospective sign-off all remain open (`docs/CURRENT_PHASE.md`; ADR-0025 commitment 1). Every model added before the deploy widens the "built-but-unused" surface ADR-0025 flagged as its central risk. An invoicing aggregate — sized against a guess about how the operator bills today, not against observed use, and on an app that has never run in production — is a real instance of that cost. It is also, like ADR-0038's channel, partly deploy-dependent: a real customer invoice is a document the operator sends from a running system, against real jobs and real PANs.

3. **This is the first revenue-side surface, and revenue + tax is a real compliance domain.** Everything to date is internal cost tracking, where a wrong number is an operator's own problem. An invoice is an **external legal/tax document**: it carries the customer's money obligation, the buyer's and supplier's PANs, and tax the operator remits to the Nepal IRD. Getting the VAT rate, the TDS treatment, the required fields, or the anti-tamper numbering wrong is not a cosmetic bug — it is a compliance exposure. **The agent must not assume the Nepal tax specifics** (commitment 9); they are stated as *proposed, pending operator/accountant verification*, in keeping with the Discipline rule that Nepal-specific compliance (BS calendar, paisa formatting, IRD integration) is operator-verified, not agent-assumed.

4. **The Trip carries no price today — so this aggregate introduces selling-price data, it does not merely read it.** ADR-0003's billing argument ("the sum of the trip-prices") assumed a trip price that the schema never added: `Trip` has odometer and time fields but **no rate or amount**. Because the cost-side aggregates never stored a *selling* price, the invoice line is the **first place a billable amount is captured** (commitment 3). This is the honest reason the invoice cannot be a pure read-and-sum of upstream money: there is no upstream selling price to sum.

The honest framing, as with ADR-0037 / ADR-0038: **this ADR proposes the pull-forward; it does not assume it.** The PO decides at acceptance whether to (a) accept and let Program D build the invoicing slice now, (b) accept but hold the implementing slice until the production deploy lands so invoices are built against a system that can actually issue them, or (c) reject the pull-forward and keep this as a `Proposed` record for when Phase 4 arrives.

## Decision

**Model invoicing as a dedicated two-table aggregate — an `Invoice` (the header: customer, optional job, the fiscal-year-scoped number, the status, the frozen tax breakdown, the R2 PDF key) and an `InvoiceLine` (the billable lines, each optionally referencing a `Trip`/`Job` by FK and carrying the captured billable amount) — rather than columns on any existing aggregate; compute Nepal VAT (13%) and the applicable TDS (1.5% vehicle-hire / 2.5% goods-transport) in integer paisa with the half-up `Math.round` rule the fuel-log derivation already uses, storing the rate and every computed amount as a frozen snapshot on the invoice; assign a sequential, gapless, never-reused invoice number scoped to the Nepali (Bikram Sambat) fiscal year at the moment of issue via a counter locked in the issuing transaction (hardening, not reusing, the `jobNumber` generator); run a `DRAFT` → `ISSUED` lifecycle where a DRAFT is fully mutable and unnumbered, issuing freezes the numbers and assigns the number, and an ISSUED invoice is immutable and corrected only by a credit note (never an edit or delete); render the invoice to a PDF through a thin FleetCo-owned interface over a proposed server-side PDF library (recommended `pdfkit`; runner-up `@react-pdf/renderer`), pinned-and-verified at install and isolated to its first code ticket; store the issued PDF in Cloudflare R2 (the first in-app R2 use); build the lines from `Customer` / `Job` / `Trip` through their public service interfaces; render every date in Bikram Sambat (ADR-0031); classify the invoice as financial Tier-3 data per ADR-0013 with the buyer PAN already covered by existing tiering; and explicitly scope real-time IRD/CBMS e-billing OUT of v1.** Ten commitments define the shape, grouped. As with its decision-record siblings (ADR-0030 / 0037 / 0038), this ADR **recommends** and leaves the genuinely owner-level picks — the storage shape, the PDF library, the numbering format, the TDS display treatment, the exact IRD field set, and the phase pull-forward — to the PO (and the operator's accountant) at acceptance.

### A. The aggregate shape — two tables, not columns

1. **A dedicated `Invoice` + `InvoiceLine` aggregate, anchored to `Customer` (and optionally `Job`) by FK (recommended; owner-level pick).** An invoice has **many** lines (one per billed trip or charge) and a customer has **many** invoices over time — a one-to-many (and one-to-many) shape that columns on `Customer` or `Job` cannot express, the same cardinality argument ADR-0037 made for `ServiceSchedule`/`ServiceRecord` and ADR-0030 made for `Geofence`. `Invoice` carries a required `customerId` FK and a **nullable** `jobId` FK (the common case is one invoice for one job, but a consolidated invoice may span several jobs or a date range of trips, and an ad-hoc invoice may reference no job). `InvoiceLine` carries a required `invoiceId` FK and a **nullable** `tripId` FK (a line is usually one trip's charge, but a manual line — a flat mobilization fee, a discount — references no trip). Every FK is `onDelete: Restrict` (the house posture — `FuelLog`, `ExpenseLog`, `GpsPing`, `Job` all Restrict): a customer or job with invoices cannot be silently deleted, and the existing `CustomersService.delete` / a `JobsService.delete` `P2003 → 409` arm covers the new referencer. The two new tables are the **only** schema additions — `Customer`, `Job`, and `Trip` are untouched except for the back-relation field Prisma requires on both sides of a relation (a model edit, not a data migration on existing rows), exactly the cost-link posture ADR-0037 commitment 6 took toward `ExpenseLog`.

### B. The billable line — capture the amount, reference the source

2. **An `InvoiceLine` references its source `Trip`/`Job` by FK for provenance and carries the billable amount on the line.** Each line stores a `description` (free text, e.g. "Haul aggregate Kalimati → Pokhara, 2083 Shrawan 3"), an integer `quantity` (or a unit count — tonnes, trips, days), an integer `unitPricePaisa`, and a derived integer `lineAmountPaisa = quantity * unitPricePaisa` (computed and stored on the line; integer-paisa, no float). The nullable `tripId`/`jobId` FKs record **where the line came from** so an invoice is traceable back to the executions it bills. **Money is not duplicated from an upstream selling price because none exists** (Phase-mismatch §4): the line *is* the first place the selling amount is recorded. What the "never duplicate money" discipline forbids here is duplicating the **cost** figures (`FuelLog.totalCostPaisa`, `ExpenseLog.amountPaisa`) into a revenue line — those are a different number for a different purpose, and the invoice neither reads nor restates them. A future trip-level rate or rate-card (so a line amount can be *derived* from a stored trip price rather than keyed) is a **named follow-up**, not v1 (commitment 10); v1 captures the amount on the line.

### C. The tax model — VAT and TDS in integer paisa, frozen at issue

3. **VAT at 13% and the applicable TDS, computed in integer paisa with half-up rounding, stored as a frozen snapshot.** The invoice's money breakdown, all integer paisa, all half-up via `Math.round` (the `deriveTotalCostPaisa` precedent):
   - `subtotalPaisa` = Σ `lineAmountPaisa` over the lines (the taxable value, before VAT; an optional `discountPaisa` may reduce it — the discounted figure is the taxable base).
   - `vatRateBp` = the VAT rate in **basis points**, `1300` for 13% — stored on the invoice so a future statutory rate change never rewrites an already-issued invoice (the anti-tamper / freeze principle, commitment 5). `vatPaisa = Math.round(taxablePaisa * vatRateBp / 10000)`.
   - `grossPaisa` = `taxablePaisa + vatPaisa` — **the amount legally billed to the customer** on the tax invoice.
   - `tdsRateBp` = the applicable TDS rate in basis points (`150` = 1.5% or `250` = 2.5%, by service type — commitment 9), and `tdsPaisa = Math.round(taxablePaisa * tdsRateBp / 10000)`. **TDS is withheld by the payer (the customer), not charged by FleetCo** — it does not change `grossPaisa`. It is captured so the invoice can show the operator the expected **net receivable** (`netReceivablePaisa = grossPaisa - tdsPaisa`, the cash FleetCo actually expects after the customer withholds and remits TDS to the IRD on FleetCo's behalf). This is the "close the loop on cash" intent of Phase 4. Whether and exactly how TDS appears **on the printed invoice** versus only in FleetCo's cash-reconciliation view is an IRD/accountant detail (commitment 9), not an agent assumption.
   - The invoice shows the prescribed breakdown: taxable value, VAT (13%), gross total, and (as a memo) the applicable TDS and net receivable.

   On **issue** (commitment 5) every one of these figures, plus the two rates, is **frozen** on the `Invoice` row. They are computed from the lines at issue time and never recomputed at render — an issued invoice's numbers are a historical fact, not a live calculation.

### D. The number and the lifecycle — gapless, fiscal-year-scoped, immutable after issue

4. **A sequential, gapless, never-reused invoice number scoped to the Nepali (BS) fiscal year, assigned at issue by a transaction-locked counter — hardening, not reusing, the `jobNumber` generator.** The number (recommended format `INV-<BS-fiscal-year>-NNNNN`, e.g. `INV-2082-83-00001`, the BS fiscal year being Shrawan→Ashadh per the glossary) is the IRD anti-tamper anchor and has three properties the `jobNumber` scheme does **not** guarantee:
   - **Gapless and never-reused.** The number is assigned **only at issue**, from a dedicated per-fiscal-year counter row read `SELECT … FOR UPDATE` inside the same Prisma interactive `$transaction` that flips the invoice to `ISSUED` — so two concurrent issues serialize on the counter and the sequence has no holes and no reuse. A discarded DRAFT never consumes a number (it has none until issue), so abandoned drafts cannot create gaps. This is the deliberate contrast with `jobNumber`'s find-highest+1+retry, which leaves holes on delete, can reuse a number when the max row is deleted, and is only race-backstopped by the unique index. (A Postgres `SEQUENCE` per fiscal year is the noted alternative — see Alternatives.)
   - **Fiscal-year-scoped by the Nepali calendar.** The counter is keyed by the **BS fiscal year** the issue date falls in (Shrawan→Ashadh), not the Gregorian year — derived via the shipped `formatNepaliDate` / BS pipeline (ADR-0031). This is a Nepal-specific compliance point (commitment 9), and the reason the number cannot simply reuse `jobNumber`'s `getUTCFullYear()`.
   - **Immutable after issue.** See the lifecycle below.

5. **A `DRAFT` → `ISSUED` lifecycle: drafts are mutable and unnumbered; issuing freezes and numbers; issued invoices are immutable and corrected only by credit note.** An `InvoiceStatus` enum (`DRAFT` / `ISSUED` / `CANCELLED`, recommended; `PAID`-tracking is a Phase-4-proper follow-up, see commitment 10):
   - A **`DRAFT`** invoice is fully editable — add/remove/edit lines, change the customer/job — and carries **no** fiscal number and **no** frozen totals. Its PDF, if previewed, is a regenerable draft watermarked as such.
   - **Issuing** is a one-way transition that, in a single `$transaction`: validates the invoice (customer present, ≥1 line, buyer PAN present if required for a VAT invoice), assigns the next gapless fiscal-year number (commitment 4), computes and **freezes** the tax snapshot (commitment 3), renders the PDF (commitment 6), stores it in R2 (commitment 7), and sets `status = ISSUED` with an `issuedAt`. After this, the row's financial fields and number are **immutable** — the `.strict()` update schema rejects every financial field on an ISSUED invoice (a parallel to `jobNumber`/`customerId` being immutable post-create on `Job`, but enforced for the whole financial body, not one field).
   - A **mistake on an issued invoice is corrected by a credit note**, never by editing or deleting the invoice. A credit note is a **separate document** (recommended: the same `Invoice` aggregate with a `documentType` discriminator `INVOICE` / `CREDIT_NOTE` and its **own** gapless fiscal-year series, referencing the original invoice by FK), so the audit trail is intact and the original number is never voided in place. `CANCELLED` is available only for a DRAFT (or an issued invoice that was never sent and is voided by an offsetting credit note); an ISSUED invoice's number is never deleted. The exact credit-note shape is an implementing-slice detail; the **commitment** is "immutable issued invoice + credit-note correction," not "editable invoice."

### E. PDF generation — a server-side library behind a thin interface, the new dependency

6. **A server-side PDF library wrapped behind a FleetCo-owned `InvoicePdfRenderer` interface (recommended: `pdfkit`; runner-up: `@react-pdf/renderer`).** *(The library-*approach* — a server-side programmatic PDF library over a headless browser or HTML-only print — is the recommendation; the specific package is the PO's owner-level pick at acceptance.)* The recommendation is **`pdfkit`**: it is mature, pure-Node, renders a fixed-layout document to a buffer with no headless browser, has no React/DOM coupling (the PDF is generated **API-side**, in NestJS, where the invoice data and the R2 client live), and a tax invoice is a fixed, table-shaped legal document, not a rich interactive page. **`@react-pdf/renderer`** is the runner-up — JSX/component templating that matches FleetCo's React/TypeScript stack and the DESIGN.md component model, the better pick if the team prefers composing the invoice as components, dispreferred only because it pulls React into the API for a server-side render. Per CLAUDE.md's "propose a new top-level dependency first," this ADR proposes the dependency; the code slice **pins an exact version and verifies at install** that it (a) imports and renders a PDF buffer under plain Node with no browser dependency, (b) ships or has `@types`, and (c) produces a byte-stable document for a fixed fixture. The library is **wrapped behind a FleetCo-owned `InvoicePdfRenderer` interface** — `render(invoice): Promise<Buffer>` — so the package is the only place that knows the vendor, exactly as `nepali-date.ts` wraps `nepali-date-converter` (ADR-0031), `apps/api/src/common/wkt.ts` centralizes the WKT builder (ADR-0030), and ADR-0038's `Mailer` wraps the email SDK: if verification fails or the PO prefers the runner-up, only the one implementation changes. The dependency is **isolated to the invoicing slice's first code ticket** for a clean blast radius, as `bullmq` was (GPS T1), Leaflet was (geofence G4), and `nepali-date-converter` was (BS N1).

### F. Storage — freeze the issued PDF in R2

7. **The issued invoice PDF is rendered once and stored in Cloudflare R2; drafts regenerate on demand.** The **exact bytes** sent to the customer are the legal artifact, so the **ISSUED** PDF is rendered at issue and **persisted to R2** (an `Invoice.pdfR2Key` column holds the object key), then served by download (a presigned/streamed read). Regenerating an issued invoice's PDF on demand is rejected: if the renderer, the template, or any referenced datum changes, a re-render would silently diverge from what the customer holds — the anti-tamper principle again. **DRAFT** previews, which have no legal standing, *are* regenerated on demand (no R2 write) and watermarked. This is the **first in-app use of R2** (ADR-0014 c6 deferred in-app R2 uploads to Phase 2; ADR-0013 commits R2 AES-256 at-rest encryption), so the implementing slice also lands the first R2 client wiring behind a thin storage interface (the same wrap-the-vendor discipline as the PDF and mail interfaces), reusable by future attachment features (Bluebook scans, receipt images).

### G. Sourcing, rendering, and data classification

8. **Lines are assembled from `Customer` / `Job` / `Trip` through their public service interfaces; dates render in Bikram Sambat.** The invoicing module reads the customer (for name + PAN), the job (for `jobNumber` + description), and the job's trips (the billable executions) **through those modules' public service interfaces / first-class Prisma FK relationships** — never by reaching into another module's repository (the CLAUDE.md cross-module rule; ADR-0037 commitment 6 drew the same line for the `ExpenseLog` cost link). The cross-aggregate assembly mirrors `ReportsService`'s read-and-merge precedent. Every date on the invoice (issue date, the trip dates on each line, the fiscal year in the number) renders through the shipped `formatNepaliDate` (ADR-0031) so the operator and the customer read the calendar they file taxes in; storage stays ISO/UTC (DESIGN.md anti-pattern #12 — never store BS).

9. **Nepal tax specifics are PROPOSED, pending operator/accountant verification (not agent-assumed); invoice data is financial Tier-3.** The following are stated as **proposed values for the PO and the operator's accountant to verify at acceptance**, per the Discipline rule that Nepal-specific compliance is operator-verified:
   - **VAT rate: 13%** (the standard Nepal VAT rate) — stored as `vatRateBp = 1300`, snapshot-frozen per invoice so a rate change is forward-only.
   - **TDS: 1.5% on vehicle-hire / 2.5% on goods-transport (carriage) within Nepal**, withheld by the payer — a `tdsRateBp` discriminator chosen by the invoice's **service type** (vehicle/equipment hire vs. goods transport). **Which service maps to which rate, and whether a given job is "hire" or "transport," is precisely the accountant-verified detail the agent must not assume.**
   - **IRD-required invoice fields:** the supplier's name + **PAN/VAT number** (FleetCo's own registration — a **company-profile/settings value the operator must supply**, distinct from the buyer's `Customer.panNumber`), the buyer's name + PAN (for a VAT-registered customer), the **"Tax Invoice" (कर बीजक) label**, the sequential invoice number, the date, the line descriptions/quantities/values, and the prescribed tax breakdown. The exact prescribed field set and labelling is IRD-specific and **operator/accountant-verified**.
   - **Gapless sequential numbering + immutability + BS fiscal year** (commitments 4–5) are stated as the IRD anti-tamper expectation, to be confirmed against current IRD rules at acceptance.
   - **Data classification:** an invoice is **Tier-3 financial / operational business data** (ADR-0013: "customer commercial terms" are Tier 3); the buyer PAN is already Tier-3 on `Customer`, and any buyer contact PII on the rendered document is the existing Tier-2 (already redacted via `*.email` / `*.phoneNumber` / `*.contactPerson`). ADR-0013's Context named this exact category ("In Phase 4 the system adds financial records that interact with Nepal's Inland Revenue Department"), and the roadmap's §"Phase 4" says the data-classification ADR "is revisited again because financial data has its own sensitivity profile and may justify additional retention or audit-logging requirements." So the implementing program should **revisit ADR-0013** (whether financial records warrant a distinct tier, longer retention, or mandatory audit-logging) as a companion decision — flagged here, decided there, not assumed.

### H. Scope boundary — real-time IRD/CBMS e-billing is OUT of v1

10. **Out of scope, stated so the boundaries are clean.** This ADR does **not** decide, and v1 does **not** build: (a) **Real-time IRD / CBMS (Central Billing Monitoring System) electronic reporting.** v1 generates **compliant printable/PDF VAT invoices**; live, real-time electronic transmission of each invoice to the IRD's CBMS is a **later, separately-scoped program** — the same deferred-delivery boundary ADR-0031 drew for reminder *delivery* and ADR-0037 drew for service-reminder *delivery*. This aligns with the roadmap's standing exclusion ("We do not build a replacement for full IRD-grade accounting… FleetCo feeds accounting, it does not replace it"): FleetCo produces correct VAT invoices; CBMS integration, when wanted, is its own ADR + program (it needs IRD enrolment, a CBMS API integration, and a compliance review). (b) **Payment/settlement tracking** (recording receipts against an invoice, partial payments, aging) — recommended as the natural next Phase-4 slice (a `PAID` status + a payments ledger), but v1 is *issue the invoice*, not *track the cash*. (c) **Trip-level pricing / a rate-card** — v1 captures the amount on the line (commitment 2); a stored trip rate is a follow-up. (d) **Lease-taker billing, vendor bills, the chart of accounts, and month-end closing** — the rest of the roadmap's Phase-4 list; this ADR is *customer invoicing* only. (e) **A generic "document" / templating abstraction** — v1 is an invoice-specific model (the EAV/over-abstraction rejection ADR-0037 made), with the credit note as a discriminator on the same aggregate, not a polymorphic document engine. (f) **The exact PDF library API, the number format string, and the IRD field layout** — recommended here, the PO's (and accountant's) to confirm at acceptance.

- **Builds on ADR-0003 (Trip as central aggregate)** — the Trip is the billable unit and the Job groups trips for a customer; this ADR realizes ADR-0003's billing argument, while honestly noting the Trip carries no price yet (Phase-mismatch §4, commitment 2).
- **Consumes the `Customer` / `Job` aggregates** — `Customer.panNumber` is the buyer tax identity, `Job.jobNumber` the work reference; read through their service interfaces (commitment 8).
- **Applies CLAUDE.md's money-as-integer-paisa rule and the fuel-log half-up precedent** (`deriveTotalCostPaisa`, `formatNpr` / `rupeesToPaisa`) to VAT/TDS math (commitment 3).
- **Hardens the `jobNumber` sequential-numbering precedent** into a gapless, fiscal-year-scoped, transaction-locked, immutable-after-issue invoice number (commitment 4) — explicitly *not* a reuse.
- **Reuses the `ReportsService` cross-aggregate `groupBy`/merge precedent** for assembling billable lines, and the ADR-0006 aggregate CRUD convention for the slice shape.
- **Reuses ADR-0031 (BS calendar)** for the Nepali fiscal year (numbering) and date rendering, and follows the dedicated-two-table-aggregate precedent of **ADR-0030 / ADR-0037**.

## Alternatives considered

**A dedicated `Invoice` + `InvoiceLine` aggregate vs. columns on `Job`/`Customer`.** Put invoice fields (number, totals, status) directly on `Job`. Rejected as the recommendation: the cardinality is wrong — a job can be invoiced in parts or consolidated with others, and an invoice has many lines and a customer has many invoices, none of which a fixed column set holds; you would invent "line 1..N" columns, the classic wrong shape. Dedicated tables are the ADR-0030 / ADR-0037 precedent for cardinality-N data.

**Tax stored as a frozen snapshot vs. recomputed at each render.** Recompute VAT/TDS (and re-sum the lines) every time the invoice is viewed/printed. Rejected decisively: an issued invoice is a legal document whose numbers must be **fixed in time** — if the VAT rate changes, a line is edited, or the rounding code changes, a recompute would silently alter a document the customer already holds and the operator already filed. Storing `vatRateBp` + every computed paisa figure on the invoice at issue (commitment 3) makes the numbers a historical fact. The cost is a few denormalized columns; the anti-tamper guarantee repays it. This is the same freeze-on-issue argument that drives the immutable-after-issue lifecycle.

**Sequential numbering: a transaction-locked per-fiscal-year counter (recommended) vs. a Postgres `SEQUENCE` per fiscal year vs. the `jobNumber` find-highest+1 pattern.** The `jobNumber` pattern is rejected for invoices (Context, commitment 4): it leaves gaps on delete, can reuse a number, and is keyed by the Gregorian year — none acceptable for an IRD anti-tamper number. A **Postgres `SEQUENCE` per fiscal year** is the strong alternative — gapless-on-success and concurrency-safe at the DB — but a sequence **advances even on a rolled-back transaction**, so a failed issue would burn a number and create a gap, and managing one sequence per BS fiscal year is its own ceremony; it is the noted fallback if counter contention ever shows up. The **recommended** scheme is a counter row read `SELECT … FOR UPDATE` inside the issuing `$transaction`: gapless even across rollbacks (the counter only advances when the issue commits), trivially fiscal-year-keyed, and using the interactive-transaction tool the codebase already uses (the Trip→Vehicle odometer bump, ADR-0037's anchor advance). **Anti-tamper posture:** immutable-issued + credit-note correction (recommended) vs. an editable invoice (rejected — an editable financial document defeats the entire point of a sequential number).

**PDF: a server-side library (recommended) vs. a headless-browser render vs. HTML-only print.** A **headless browser** (Puppeteer/Playwright rendering an HTML template to PDF) gives the richest layout and reuses web/CSS skills, but it ships a whole Chromium — heavy and operationally fragile on the ADR-0014 single-VPS box, and overkill for a fixed-layout document; rejected. **HTML-only print** (just style a web page for the browser's print dialog) adds no dependency but produces **no durable artifact** to store, version, or attach — unacceptable for a document that must be frozen and archived (commitment 7); rejected. A **server-side library** (`pdfkit` / `@react-pdf/renderer`) produces a byte-stable buffer in-process, API-side, with a modest dependency — the right fit, and the one wrapped behind the `InvoicePdfRenderer` interface so the specific choice stays swappable. (`pdf-lib` was considered and set aside: it excels at *manipulating existing* PDFs, less at composing a tabular document from scratch.)

**Store the rendered PDF in R2 vs. regenerate on demand.** Regenerate-on-demand saves a write and a column. Rejected for **issued** invoices (commitment 7): the bytes the customer holds are the legal artifact and must not drift if the renderer or data changes; the issued PDF is rendered once and frozen in R2. Drafts, which have no legal standing, *are* regenerated on demand — the recommendation splits by status rather than picking one rule for both.

**An invoice-specific model vs. a generic "document" / templating abstraction.** A polymorphic document engine (templates × document-types × a rules layer) is the over-engineered path — the EAV/over-abstraction anti-pattern ADR-0037 rejected for maintenance intervals. v1 is an **invoice-specific** model, with the credit note expressed as a `documentType` discriminator on the same aggregate (commitment 5) rather than a speculative generic engine. A general document system, if ever warranted, is a much later and separately-justified thing.

**Bill from a stored trip price vs. capture the amount on the invoice line.** Add a price/rate to `Trip` first, then have the invoice line *derive* its amount from it. A cleaner long-term model — but it is a change to the **central `Trip` aggregate** (its own ADR-0003-touching decision) and a larger pull-forward than invoicing needs in v1; rejected for v1 in favor of capturing the amount on the line (commitment 2), with trip-level pricing named as a follow-up (commitment 10). This keeps the v1 schema change to the two new invoice tables and leaves the Trip untouched.

**Build the invoicing schema now without a separate ADR.** Rejected on CLAUDE.md step 4: a new revenue-side aggregate built from the central Trip chain, a new top-level dependency, the first in-app R2 use, a real Nepal tax-compliance surface, and a two-phase pull-forward are exactly the changes that warrant PO confirmation before code. The ADR is that gate, mirroring ADR-0029 (storage before GPS code), ADR-0031 (dependency before BS code), ADR-0037 (shape before maintenance code), and ADR-0038 (shape + dependency before notification code).

## Consequences

### What this makes easier

FleetCo gains its **first revenue-side surface**: the operator can turn a completed job's trips into a compliant Nepal VAT invoice — with the buyer's PAN, the 13% VAT, the applicable TDS, a sequential anti-tamper number in the right fiscal year, and a stored PDF — instead of hand-writing it in a spreadsheet. Because the design is "the dedicated-aggregate + integer-paisa + freeze-on-issue patterns the project already runs," the implementing slice is mostly composition of proven parts: the two-table aggregate copies ADR-0037's shape, the tax math is the fuel-log half-up rule applied to a new computation, the numbering hardens the `jobNumber` precedent, the dates reuse `formatNepaliDate`, the cross-aggregate read mirrors `ReportsService`, and the PDF library hides behind a one-method interface like every prior new dependency. It also lays the financial foundation the rest of Phase 4 (payments, lease-taker billing, chart of accounts, IRD-friendly exports) builds on. And it is legible memory: one ADR explains how a job becomes a billed invoice, why the number is gapless and the issued invoice immutable, why VAT/TDS live in integer paisa, why the PDF is frozen in R2, and what is deliberately left for later (CBMS, payments, trip pricing).

### What this makes harder

A new **external, legal/tax document** enters the system — the first artifact whose correctness is a *compliance* matter, not just an operator's convenience. The tax math, the field set, the TDS treatment, and the numbering rules must be **right by Nepal IRD standards**, which means an operator/accountant verification gate this codebase has not needed before (commitment 9). The immutable-after-issue invariant is a new standing discipline: every write path must refuse to mutate an issued invoice's financials, and corrections must route through credit notes — a contributor who "just fixes" an issued total reintroduces exactly the tamper risk the design prevents. The gapless counter is a new concurrency-sensitive write (the `SELECT … FOR UPDATE` in the issue transaction) that must stay correct under contention. And a new vendor dependency (the PDF library) plus the first in-app R2 client join the surfaces whose majors and limits must be watched.

### Costs we accept

- **A revenue aggregate set without production telemetry, two phases ahead, on an undeployed app (ADR-0025).** The invoice shape, the line model, and the issue flow are sized against assumption about how a Nepali heavy-fleet operator bills, not against observed use — and this is the **furthest pull-forward yet** (Phase 4 from a Phase-2 present). We accept this as the explicit, PO-decided cost of the pull-forward; it is reversible in that an *unaccepted* ADR ships nothing, and the PO may reasonably choose to hold the implementing slice until the deploy lands so invoices are built against a system that can issue them.
- **Tax correctness depends on operator/accountant verification, not the agent.** The VAT rate, the TDS rates and their service mapping, the required IRD fields, and the anti-tamper rules are stated as proposed-pending-verification (commitment 9). We accept that the implementing slice cannot proceed on the agent's say-so for these — they are a gated input, the right posture for a compliance surface.
- **Real-time CBMS is deferred.** v1 produces correct invoices but does not report them live to the IRD (commitment 10). An operator who needs CBMS e-billing has a gap until that separate program lands; we accept this by design, matching the roadmap's "feeds accounting, does not replace it" boundary, rather than pull an even larger integration forward.
- **Selling price is captured per-line, not modelled on the Trip.** Until a trip-rate/rate-card follow-up lands, the same job billed twice could be keyed at two different amounts (no single source of the trip price). We accept this v1 simplification to keep the schema change to the two invoice tables and the central Trip aggregate untouched.

## Revisit when

- **PO acceptance fixes the open picks.** Acceptance ratifies the storage shape (the recommended dedicated `Invoice` + `InvoiceLine` aggregate), the PDF library (recommended `pdfkit`, runner-up `@react-pdf/renderer`), the number format and the counter-vs-sequence numbering mechanism, the TDS on-invoice display treatment, the `InvoiceStatus` set, and the phase pull-forward. If the PO chooses differently, the implementing slice follows the PO's pick and this ADR is annotated.
- **The operator's accountant verifies the Nepal tax specifics.** The VAT rate (13%), the TDS rates (1.5% / 2.5%) and their service mapping, the IRD-required field set and labelling (incl. FleetCo's own supplier PAN as a configured value), and the gapless/immutable/BS-fiscal-year numbering rules are confirmed against current IRD rules **before** the implementing slice builds (commitment 9). This is the compliance gate the whole aggregate hangs on.
- **The PO rules on the phase pull-forward and the deploy ordering.** Acceptance is where the PO decides whether the invoicing slice proceeds now, waits for the production deploy so invoices are issued from a running system, or is held. A two-phase pull-forward onto an undeployed Phase 1 is the ADR-0025 "built-but-unused" risk at its largest; if it accumulates, that is the signal the deploy must precede further pull-forwards.
- **ADR-0013 is revisited for financial data.** The roadmap's Phase 4 anticipates re-examining data classification for financial records' sensitivity profile (retention, audit-logging); confirm whether invoices warrant a distinct tier or mandatory audit-logging as a companion decision to the implementing program (commitment 9).
- **Payments / settlement tracking is wanted.** The trigger to add a `PAID` status + a payments ledger against invoices (commitment 10) — the natural next Phase-4 slice once invoices exist.
- **Real-time CBMS / IRD e-billing is wanted.** The trigger for its own ADR + program — IRD enrolment, a CBMS API integration, and a compliance review (commitment 10).
- **Trip-level pricing / a rate-card is wanted.** The trigger to add a stored trip price so a line amount derives from it rather than being keyed (commitments 2, 10) — a change that touches the central `Trip` aggregate and so is its own decision.
- **The PDF library majors, or R2 in-app usage grows.** Re-verify the PDF library at the bump (the `InvoicePdfRenderer` interface keeps the blast radius at its one implementation); as more in-app R2 features land (attachments, scans), promote the first-use R2 client wiring into a shared storage module.
