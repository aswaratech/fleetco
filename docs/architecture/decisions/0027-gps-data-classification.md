# ADR-0027: GPS and location-data classification — amend ADR-0013 with a sensitive-location Tier 5, short raw-trace retention, precision/aggregation controls, and never-export egress

- **Status:** Proposed
- **Date:** 2026-06-03
- **Decider:** Product owner (CEO) — pending acceptance

## Context

Phase 2 ("Driver app and telematics") is open per ADR-0025. Its headline new data is the GPS trace: a React Native (Expo) driver app streams background location, the system ingests pings through BullMQ jobs, stores them in Postgres+PostGIS, and derives geofence crossings for the depot, customer sites, and route corridors (`docs/product/roadmap.md` §"Phase 2"; glossary entries **Geofence**, **PostGIS**, **BullMQ**). This is the most sensitive data category FleetCo has yet held, and it arrives in the highest volume.

ADR-0013 set FleetCo's data-classification and privacy posture with a **four-tier model** (Tier 1 administrative/auth, Tier 2 driver/customer PII, Tier 3 operational business data, Tier 4 non-sensitive metadata) and per-tier rules for logging, encryption, retention, and access. ADR-0013 saw this moment coming. Its Context already singled GPS out — "a GPS trace reveals the work patterns of the company and the location patterns of the driver" — and its **"Revisit when"** names the exact trigger now firing: "The Phase 2 introduction of GPS traces is a candidate for revisiting because GPS traces have privacy properties that may justify a fifth tier or a tighter retention policy." The roadmap restates the same expectation: the ADR-0013 classification "is revisited because GPS traces are personally identifying data of a particularly sensitive kind; this likely produces a Phase 2 amendment to ADR-0013 with tighter retention and possibly an additional tier." **This ADR is that amendment.** It is the second of three Phase-2 foundation ADRs proposed together: ADR-0026 (observability upgrade, just merged) and ADR-0028 (office-staff RBAC, proposed next) are its siblings, and this ADR forward-references both where they bind on it.

ADR-0013 is **append-only and stays in place**. This ADR does not edit it; it **amends by superseding and extending specific points**, enumerated in the "Relationship to ADR-0013" subsection below, so a future reader of ADR-0013 who follows its "Revisit when" lands here and learns exactly what changed.

Two constraints shape the choice. First, **cost and topology (ADR-0014):** production is a single 2–4 GB VPS under Docker Compose, operated by one person. Across a fleet, GPS pings are by a wide margin the highest-row-count table FleetCo will hold — keeping them at full resolution forever is both a privacy liability and a standing threat to the disk budget of a single box. Second, **the egress seam this binds (ADR-0026).** ADR-0026 commitment 5 mandates a span-attribute scrub before any trace leaves the process, and it deliberately left the GPS category's exact handling to this ADR: "their tier is set by ADR-0027 … this scrub list must honor whatever tier ADR-0027 assigns … The default posture until then: GPS coordinates never appear in an exported span attribute." This ADR finalizes that tier; the scrub denylist and the pino `redact` list are the two layers that enforce it. There is also a Nepal-specific dimension: continuous tracking of an employee's location implicates the driver's privacy interest under Nepal's **Individual Privacy Act 2018** (the same statute ADR-0013 cited), which data minimization, collection-scoping, and shortened retention are the mitigations for.

This ADR **decides classification and policy only**. It writes no schema, no migration, no redact-list code, and no scrub denylist code — those land with the GPS/telematics schema slice and the trace turn-on ticket, which consume the policy fixed here.

## Decision

**Introduce a new Tier 5 — "Sensitive location / telematics data" — for raw GPS pings and full-resolution traces, with controls at least as strict as Tier 2 plus four location-specific commitments: collection scoped to on-shift/on-trip windows, a short raw-trace retention window (provisionally 90 days) after which raw pings are pruned or aggregated to Tier-3 derived summaries, native precision in the operational window with aggregation (not blanket coarsening) as the primary minimization lever, and never-logged / never-exported egress enforced at both the pino `redact` and the ADR-0026 span-scrub layers; derived geofence-crossing events and trip route summaries are classified Tier 3, and raw-trace access is the most-privileged data class, with the role mapping deferred to ADR-0028.** Eight commitments define the shape. As with ADR-0026, this ADR fixes decisions and **writes no code**; the implementing tickets build them.

1. **A new Tier 5: "Sensitive location / telematics data."** It covers raw GPS pings (latitude/longitude/altitude/speed/heading/timestamp), full-resolution stored traces, and the background-location stream from the driver app. Tier 5 inherits **all of Tier 2's controls** — encrypted at rest, **never logged by default**, access restricted to authenticated users on a need-to-know basis — and **adds** the four location-specific controls in commitments 2–5. **On the numbering:** ADR-0013's tiers run in descending protection (Tier 1 most protected → Tier 4 least), so "Tier 5" is the next free *label*, not a claim that it is less sensitive than Tier 4. In protection terms the ranking is **Tier 1 ≥ Tier 5 ≈ Tier 2 > Tier 3 > Tier 4**: Tier 5 is best understood as a **specialized, stricter sibling of Tier 2** (location *is* a kind of PII) carved out as its own category because its retention, precision, and access controls differ enough from generic name/license-number PII that folding them into Tier 2 would either leave them undefined or distort Tier 2 for data it does not govern. ADR-0013 explicitly permitted this: it rejected a five-tier model as "premature" but added "the scheme can be expanded if a future module introduces a kind of data the existing tiers do not address" — Phase-2 telematics is exactly that module.

2. **Collection is scoped to on-shift / on-trip windows — data minimization at the source.** The strongest privacy control for location data is not collecting what is not needed. The driver app streams location **only while a trip is active (driver on shift)** and stops when the trip ends; it does not track the driver off-duty, and it tracks the *work use of a company vehicle*, not the driver's personal time. This honors ADR-0013's data-minimization principle ("we collect only the data we have a clear operational need for, we do not collect data 'just in case'") applied to the most invasive collection FleetCo does, and it is also a battery and quota win. This commitment is a **classification/privacy requirement the driver-app slice must satisfy**; the app's exact offline/queueing mechanics are that slice's to design.

3. **Short raw-trace retention; raw pings pruned or aggregated to Tier-3 derived summaries.** Tier 2/3 retain for "the lifetime of the customer relationship plus three years" (ADR-0013). That window is **wrong for raw pings** — a multi-year, meter-resolution behavioral trail of a named driver is surveillance-grade data with little operational value past the near term. The decision: **raw Tier-5 pings are retained for a short window — provisionally 90 days (the PO sets the final number at acceptance)** — sized to cover realistic operational lookback (dispute resolution, fuel-theft investigation, billing reconciliation). At the end of the window, raw pings are **either hard-deleted or down-sampled/aggregated into a derived trip summary** (a simplified route polyline, total distance, and stop/geofence events). The **derived summary is Tier 3** and inherits the ordinary operational retention; the surveillance-grade raw trail does not persist. This also serves ADR-0014: the highest-row-count table in the system does not grow without bound on a single VPS's disk. (Note the interaction with ADR-0013's 30-day backup pruning: raw pings age out of backups at 30 days regardless; the 90-day figure governs the primary store.)

4. **Native precision in the operational window; aggregation, not blanket coarsening, is the primary lever.** Raw pings are stored at **native GPS precision during the operational window** because geofencing (a depot gate, a customer-site boundary) needs meter-level accuracy and route reconstruction needs full fidelity — truncating coordinates on ingest would silently degrade the very features Phase 2 is built to deliver. Minimization is therefore applied through **retention and aggregation (commitment 3)**, not by keeping a long-lived but coarsened trail — because a coarsened trail is still a trail. Where coarsening *is* used (e.g. a long-retained analytics layer, should one be built), the target is **≤ 3 decimal degrees (~110 m)**, enough for area-level patterns and not for doorstep-level re-identification. The default and recommended posture is **aggregate-or-delete over keep-but-coarsen**.

5. **Never logged, never exported — Tier-1-grade egress exclusion at both layers.** GPS coordinates and geometry are treated like Tier 1 for egress: **they never appear in logs (even at debug) and never leave the process in a trace span.** This is enforced at the two layers that are the same "no sensitive data leaves the process" rule expressed twice:
   - **Logs:** the pino `redact` list in `apps/api/src/app.module.ts` (today: `authorization`, `cookie`, `set-cookie`, `*.password`, `*.token`, `*.secret`, `*.email`, `*.driverName`, `*.licenseNumber`, `*.phoneNumber`, `*.contactPerson`) gains GPS attribute paths.
   - **Traces:** the ADR-0026 commitment-5 span-scrub denylist's "GPS coordinates / geometry" category is **finalized against this tier** — Tier 5 ⇒ never exported, matching ADR-0026's stated default.

   The two lists must stay **in sync** — drift between them is the failure mode, exactly as ADR-0026 framed it (and as Tailwind-vs-DESIGN.md drift is for the design system). The **exact attribute keys land with the GPS schema slice** (no redact/scrub code changes in this ADR); the provisional set the implementing tickets will cover is `latitude`, `longitude`, `lat`, `lng`, `lon`, `altitude`, `heading`, `speed`, `coordinates`, `geometry`, `location`, `point`, `position`, and any geofence polygon geometry. Per ADR-0026 commitment 5, **traces do not export to a real backend until that scrub seam exists and is tested** — this tier is its specification.

6. **Derived geofence-crossing events and trip summaries are Tier 3 — with an anti-circumvention clause.** A discrete geofence-crossing event ("vehicle X entered the depot geofence at 14:32") and a trip-level route summary reveal far less than a continuous trail: a business-meaningful enter/exit at a *company-defined* location, not a meter-by-meter record of everywhere the vehicle went. These derived products are **Tier 3 operational business data**, like trip records. **Geofence polygons themselves** (depot, customer site, route corridor) are also **Tier 3** operational configuration — they are company assets, and a customer-site boundary is already Tier-3 customer data. The **anti-circumvention clause:** a "derived" product that is dense enough to reconstruct the raw trail (e.g. a crossing event per ping, or a near-full-resolution polyline) does **not** earn the Tier-3 reduction — it remains Tier 5. Derivation lowers the tier only when it genuinely lowers resolution and identifiability; you cannot launder Tier-5 data into Tier 3 by relabeling it.

7. **Raw-trace access is the most-privileged data class; the role mapping is ADR-0028's.** Access to **raw Tier-5 traces** (a queryable, exportable, full-resolution history of a named driver's movements) is the **most privileged operational data access in the system** and is governed by need-to-know. The default posture for Phase 2: **raw-trace query/export is CEO/ADMIN-only**; office staff get **derived, operational views** (a live current-location map, geofence status, a trip's route summary) but **not** raw-trail export. The **exact role→permission mapping is ADR-0028's to set** (sibling, proposed next) — ADR-0028 must include a permission for raw-trace access distinct from operational/derived views; this ADR fixes the *requirement* that the two are different privilege levels, not the mechanism. A driver's right to their own data is unchanged: ADR-0013's data-subject-request commitment and its runbook procedure cover a driver asking for or to delete their own trace.

8. **Marking-convention extension: `/// Tier 5 per ADR-0027 — …`.** ADR-0013's schema convention — every sensitive column carries a triple-slash `/// Tier N per ADR-0013 — <description>` so a future contributor or agent knows the handling rules before reading a line of logic — extends to Tier 5, citing **this ADR** (since this ADR defines the tier). Future telematics columns are marked, for example:

   ```prisma
   /// Tier 5 per ADR-0027 — raw GPS latitude (WGS84). Never logged (pino redact)
   /// and never exported in a span (ADR-0026 scrub); raw pings pruned/aggregated
   /// after the retention window.
   latitude  Float
   ```

   Derived geofence-event and trip-summary columns stay `/// Tier 3 per ADR-0013 — …` per commitment 6. The marking is the schema-layer half of the "path-as-meaning" memory discipline; the redact/scrub lists are the runtime half.

### Relationship to ADR-0013 (what this amends)

ADR-0013 stays in place and is not edited. This ADR amends it at these specific points:

- **Extends** the four-tier scheme (ADR-0013 Decision ¶1) to **five tiers** by adding Tier 5. Tiers 1–4 are unchanged.
- **Supersedes** ADR-0013's Alternatives conclusion that "four tiers cover the data sensitivity we currently see" and its rejection of a five-tier model — on the exact ground ADR-0013 itself reserved ("the scheme can be expanded if a future module introduces a kind of data the existing tiers do not address"). This is ADR-0013 operating as designed, not being overruled.
- **Supersedes**, for raw Tier-5 traces only, the retention rule that "Tier 2 and Tier 3 data [is retained] for … the lifetime of the customer relationship plus … three years" (ADR-0013 Decision ¶2). Raw pings get the short window of commitment 3; the lifetime+3yr rule stands for Tiers 2 and 3, including Tier-3 derived location summaries.
- **Extends** ADR-0013's access rule (Tier-3 "access follows the role-based access control rules to be defined in Phase 2") by requiring raw-trace access to be the most-privileged class, with the mapping deferred to ADR-0028 (commitment 7).
- **Discharges** ADR-0013's "Revisit when" trigger naming GPS as a candidate for a fifth tier / tighter retention. That revisit is now done; this ADR is its outcome.

## Alternatives considered

**Reclassify GPS into the existing Tier 2 (PII) rather than create Tier 5.** Considered and rejected as the primary, though it is the closest alternative — location data genuinely *is* a kind of PII. It fails because Tier 2's controls are necessary but not sufficient: Tier 2 says nothing about a *shorter* retention (its rule is the lifetime+3yr that is actively wrong for raw pings), nothing about precision/aggregation, and nothing about the highest-privilege access split for raw vs. derived. Folding GPS into Tier 2 would either leave those controls silently undefined or force Tier 2 to grow controls that do not apply to a driver's name or license number — distorting the tier for the data it already governs. A dedicated category keeps each tier's rules coherent. Tier 5 is defined as "Tier 2 plus location controls" precisely to keep the PII lineage explicit while separating the controls that differ.

**Keep four tiers and handle GPS with ad-hoc per-field rules.** Rejected. ADR-0013's whole point is that data handling follows from a small, legible classification, not from scattered per-field judgment calls that the next agent cannot reconstruct. GPS is the most sensitive data in the system; leaving it un-tiered and governed by comments would be the largest gap in the classification, not a place to economize. ADR-0013 also warned that classifying data *after* it exists is far costlier than before — Tier 5 is being set before the first ping is stored, which is the cheap moment.

**Retain raw pings under the standard lifetime+3yr retention (treat them like Tier 3 operational data).** Rejected on privacy and cost. A multi-year, full-resolution trail of where a named driver was every few seconds is surveillance-grade data whose operational value decays to near zero within months, while its privacy liability (and its row count on a single VPS) only grows. Commitment 3's short window plus aggregate-or-delete keeps the durable operational value (which trips ran, rough route, distance, stops) as Tier-3 derived data and lets the raw trail expire.

**Truncate/coarsen coordinates on ingest to reduce sensitivity up front.** Rejected as the primary lever. Geofencing and route reconstruction need native precision in the operational window; coarsening on ingest would silently break the features Phase 2 exists to ship. Minimization is better applied through retention and aggregation (a coarsened trail is still a trail). Coarsening survives only as a secondary control for any long-retained analytics layer, at ≤ 3 decimal degrees (commitment 4).

**Scrub GPS only at the trace layer (lean on ADR-0026's span scrub) and not also in the pino `redact` list.** Rejected. The two egress paths — structured logs and exported spans — are independent; a coordinate that is scrubbed from a span can still reach a log line, and vice versa. Tier-1-grade exclusion means *neither* path emits it, so both lists must carry the GPS keys and must stay in sync. This mirrors ADR-0026's own framing of the redact/scrub denylist as one rule expressed at two layers.

**Defer the whole classification until the GPS schema slice is built.** Rejected for the same reason ADR-0026 was not deferred until trace turn-on: the schema slice should be built *against* a settled classification, not invent one inline under delivery pressure. The marking convention (commitment 8), the retention shape, and the egress rules are exactly the inputs the schema and ingestion code consume; fixing them now means the implementing slice copies a pattern instead of relitigating a policy. It also keeps ADR-0026's scrub seam unblocked — that seam cannot be finalized until this tier exists.

## Consequences

### What this makes easier

The GPS/telematics schema slice arrives with its handling rules already settled: every coordinate column gets a `Tier 5 per ADR-0027` mark, the ingestion path knows raw pings are short-lived and derived summaries are Tier 3, and the redact/scrub lists have a named, finalized denylist to implement. ADR-0026's scrub seam is unblocked — its GPS category now has the tier it was waiting on, so traces can be turned on (with GPS never exported) once the seam is built. The privacy posture for the single most sensitive data category is defensible and legible: a future agent, a future PO, or a customer asking about data handling can read one ADR and understand what FleetCo collects, for how long, who can see it, and why. The short raw-retention window also protects the single VPS's disk budget (ADR-0014) as a side effect, aligning the privacy lever and the cost lever.

### What this makes harder

A new data-lifecycle obligation enters the system: the **prune/aggregate job** that enforces the raw-trace retention window is now a thing that must exist, run reliably, and be monitored — likely itself a BullMQ scheduled job, and a candidate for one of Phase 2's new traces and metrics. Every new telematics attribute added from here on must be checked against the Tier-5 marking convention and the redact/scrub denylists, and the two denylists must be kept in sync — a permanent correctness obligation, the same standing cost ADR-0026 accepted for its scrub seam. The on-shift-only collection scoping (commitment 2) constrains the driver app's design — it cannot take the simpler "always stream" path — and the derived-vs-raw access split (commitment 7) gives ADR-0028 a concrete permission it must model.

### Costs we accept

- **The 90-day raw-retention window is provisional and set without production data (ADR-0025).** Like ADR-0026's provisional SLI targets, the number is sized against assumption, not measured operational lookback. We accept this and mark it for calibration once real telematics use exists.
- **A short raw window means some lookback is irrecoverable.** If an investigation needs full-resolution movement older than the window, and the trail was deleted rather than aggregated, that detail is gone. This is the deliberate trade for not holding surveillance-grade data indefinitely; the derived summary preserves the operational essentials.
- **Aggregation is real work that does not exist yet.** "Aggregate to a derived summary" is a feature (polyline simplification, stop detection) the telematics slice must build if the PO prefers aggregation over hard deletion at window's end; until it exists, the fallback is deletion. The ADR fixes the policy and names the cost; it does not pretend the aggregation code is free.

## Revisit when

- **A Nepali regulatory change materially affects location-data handling** — a strengthening of the Individual Privacy Act 2018, sectoral telematics/employee-monitoring rules, or a binding data-localization requirement — revisit retention, the export-to-SaaS posture (which ADR-0026's external observability backend already raises), and consent/notice obligations to the driver.
- **Real telematics volume and operational use exist (after the deferred deploy, ADR-0025).** Calibrate the 90-day raw window against how far back investigations actually reach, and revisit whether aggregation or deletion is the right end-of-window action.
- **ADR-0028 sets the office-staff role model.** Finalize the raw-vs-derived access permissions against the concrete roles ADR-0028 defines; if it grants office staff any raw-trace access, that change is governed there and reflected back here.
- **A new location-bearing data category appears** that Tier 5 does not cleanly fit (e.g. driver biometric/health telematics, dashcam media with bystanders in frame, or third-party location feeds), revisit whether Tier 5 stretches to cover it or a further category is warranted — applying the same test ADR-0013 set: does an existing tier already address this kind of data?
- **The driver-app offline/queueing design forces a collection-scoping question** that commitment 2 left to that slice (e.g. whether a queued ping captured at shift end but delivered later is in or out of scope) — settle it against this ADR's minimization intent rather than silently.
