# ADR-0047: Trip dispatch + driver acceptance — the haulage order on the Trip aggregate

- **Status:** Accepted (ratified by the PO — the W1 governance PR #231 and the full W1–W9 dispatch program were merged 2026-07-12/13, with the deploy-first recommendation surfaced and declined per §Phase mismatch. This status line was flipped from Proposed under explicit PO authorization on 2026-07-13, reflecting the ratification the #231 merge already effected — per the ADR-0038 rule that the PO ratifies separately.)
- **Date:** 2026-07-12
- **Decider:** Product owner (CEO)

## Context

FleetCo's central aggregate is the Trip (ADR-0003). Today a Trip is a **skeleton**: `vehicleId`, `driverId`, `status` (`PLANNED → IN_PROGRESS → COMPLETED`, `CANCELLED` from either non-terminal state), start/end odometer + engine-hours, start/end times, and free-text `notes`. The admin creates a `PLANNED` trip and the driver simply **Starts** it (a PATCH → `IN_PROGRESS`) and **Stops** it (→ `COMPLETED`).

What a haulage trip *is* — haul **which material**, **from where** (a crusher/pit), **to where** (a delivery site), **for whom** (a consignee at the drop-off) — exists nowhere in the schema. It survives only as unstructured prose in `Job.description`, whose own doc-comment example is literally *"Haul aggregate from Kalimati to Pokhara site."* The `Trip → Job` link that ADR-0003 and the roadmap both describe (*"a Job is a contract… the Trips are the executions of that contract"*) was **designed but never built** — `Trip` has no `jobId` (a standing `docs/tech-debt.md` entry).

The PO wants the real operating workflow, modelled on a **ride-hailing driver interface** but with every order coming from the internal admin app (there are **no public users**): the admin **dispatches** a trip to a specific driver + vehicle carrying the order details; the driver receives a **request** in the phone app and **accepts** it; then drives it, tapping progress as they go. This is the operator's day-to-day reality — a tipper is told "go to Kalimati crusher, load gravel, deliver to the Pokhara site, ask for Ram (98…)" — and FleetCo cannot represent it.

**Why this is a legitimate reshape and not gold-plating.** ADR-0003 explicitly reserved this: *"The Trip schema gets more careful design attention than any other schema… we are willing to revisit and reshape it… The Trip is the first place we will ADR-revise if it turns out our model of trips is wrong."* The current model is not wrong so much as **unfinished** — it never carried the order it exists to execute. This ADR finishes that.

**Governance.** "Job assignment and dispatch UI" is a named **Phase-5** roadmap deliverable, and this reshapes the central aggregate, so under **ADR-0041 commitment 4** it needs a fresh PO-ratified ADR arguing the case afresh (the mechanism ADR-0042/0043/0044-0045/0046 each used). A near-identical request — a *standing* vehicle↔driver assignment — was PO-deferred on 2026-07-05 to keep the first deploy first; that item is **distinct** from this one (a persistent pairing, not per-journey dispatch — `docs/tech-debt.md` says so explicitly) and stays deferred. The PO, with the deploy-first recommendation surfaced and declined, chose to build this now: reshaping the central Trip model is far cheaper **before** real production trip data exists than after, and the deploy is operator-gated, not agent-gated. **All production use gates on the M1 first deploy** like every prior exception; this program does not delay the deploy or the in-flight driver-app GPS stream (ADR-0035 D5/D6). See §Phase mismatch.

The design is grounded in how dispatch/haulage systems actually work (courier TMS + ride-hailing + aggregate-hauler ticketing): **hard-assign + driver acknowledge** (not marketplace auto-matching), **one trip = one load**, and intra-trip progress tracked as **timestamps, not a status explosion**.

## Decision

Extend the central Trip aggregate with a dispatch → acceptance lifecycle and a structured haulage order, introduce a reusable pinned-location aggregate for pickup/drop-off, and give the driver app a request → accept → progress flow — **completing, not replacing, ADR-0003's Trip**. Twelve commitments.

1. **The status machine gains two states; progress is timestamps.** `TripStatus` extends to `PLANNED → OFFERED → ACCEPTED → IN_PROGRESS → COMPLETED`, with `CANCELLED` reachable from any non-terminal state. **OFFERED** = dispatched to a specific driver + vehicle, awaiting the driver. **ACCEPTED** = the driver acknowledged; not yet started. Intra-trip stages (en route to pickup, loaded, en route to drop-off, delivered) are **nullable timestamps on the Trip**, not new statuses — the single most important anti-over-engineering lesson from every dispatch system reviewed, and consistent with FleetCo's ISO/UTC-dates convention. **One trip = one load** (a multi-load job is many trips; not modelled as loads-within-a-trip). The enum lives in **five lock-step mirrors** that must move together: the Prisma enum (`schema.prisma`), `TRIP_STATUSES` and `TRIP_STATUS_TRANSITIONS` (`trips.schemas.ts`), the driver-app union (`apps-mobile/driver/src/trips.ts`), and the web `types.ts` vocabulary.

2. **Accept-only — no in-app decline (PO decision).** The driver **Accepts** (`OFFERED → ACCEPTED`); there is no in-app decline. A driver who cannot take a trip is handled out-of-band, and the admin **reassigns** by editing the OFFERED/ACCEPTED trip's driver/vehicle (the existing PATCH re-assign path — vehicle/driver are re-connectable on any non-terminal trip). No `DECLINED` status, no auto-matching, no offer-cascade, no acceptance-countdown: correct for a single internal fleet where the dispatcher controls allocation, and materially simpler than a marketplace matcher.

3. **The order lives on the Trip as columns (cardinality-1).** Because dispatch:trip is 1:1, the order attaches as **nullable columns on Trip**, following the ADR-0036 precedent (engine-hours chose columns-on-Trip over a side table for cardinality-one data) rather than a separate `Dispatch` child aggregate. New Trip fields: `materialType` (enum, c5) + `materialNote` (free text for `OTHER`), `pickupSiteId` / `dropoffSiteId` (FK → `Site`, c4), `consigneeName` + `consigneePhone` (c6), `expectedLoadCount` (Int?), `specialInstructions` (String?), `docketNumber` (String?, the free-text weigh-ticket/BOL key — no weighbridge integration), and the nullable milestone timestamps `offeredAt`, `acceptedAt`, `arrivedPickupAt`, `loadedAt`, `arrivedDropoffAt`, `deliveredAt`. The order is **required at the `→ OFFERED` transition** (material + pickup + drop-off), unconstrained before it — mirroring how meter readings are required only at `IN_PROGRESS`/`COMPLETED` today. The `.strict()` Create/Update Zod schemas mean every field is added to both or the request 400s.

4. **Pinned locations are a new reusable `Site` aggregate (PO chose ride-sharing-style map pins).** Pickup (crusher/pit) and drop-off (site) are **geographic pins**, not free text and not the polygon `Geofence` (wrong grain — a boundary, no crusher type, customer-owned). A new `Site` model reuses the ADR-0029 GpsPing **hybrid Point pattern**: native `latitude`/`longitude` `Float` (canonical) + a generated `geometry(Point, 4326)` column with a GIST index, hand-authored in the migration (`ST_SetSRID(ST_MakePoint(longitude, latitude), 4326)` — X = lon, Y = lat), `Unsupported`-declared so Prisma never selects it. Fields: `name`, `kind` (`SiteKind`: `CRUSHER` / `PIT` / `DELIVERY_SITE` / `DEPOT` / `OTHER`), `latitude`, `longitude`, optional `address`, optional `contactName` / `contactPhone` (site-level contact, Tier-2). A dispatch **picks a saved Site from a list or drops a new pin on the map** (ad-hoc → saved as a `Site`). "Define once, reuse forever" fits a single company that repeatedly hauls the same lanes, and the point geometry sets up map rendering, distance, and a **later** optional `Site.geofenceId?` link (ADR-0030) for automatic arrival detection — explicitly deferred. New `sites:*` capability (ADMIN + OFFICE_STAFF), the Customer/Geofence module pattern; `pickupSiteId`/`dropoffSiteId` use `onDelete: Restrict` (a Site referenced by a trip cannot be silently deleted → P2003 → 409, the house delete-blocker).

5. **Material is a curated enum with an escape hatch.** `MaterialType` seeds the common Nepal aggregate-haulage materials (e.g. `SAND`, `AGGREGATE`, `STONE`, `BOULDER`, `GRAVEL`, `SOIL`, `BRICKS`, `OTHER`) as a Prisma enum; `OTHER` pairs with a free-text `materialNote`. An enum (not a managed lookup aggregate) keeps v1 lean and is trivially extendable by a later migration; if the material list starts needing per-row attributes (density, default rate) it graduates to its own aggregate — a named revisit, not a v1 concern.

6. **Consignee + site contacts are Tier-2 PII (ADR-0013 amendment).** `consigneeName`/`consigneePhone` (the drop-off receiver — generally *not* the billing Customer, so **not** shoehorned into the Customer aggregate) and `Site.contactName`/`contactPhone` are individual-person identifiers → **Tier 2**. The pino redact paths in `apps/api/src/observability/log-redact.ts` already cover `*.phone` (so `consigneePhone`/`contactPhone` inherit redaction) and `*.contactPerson`; a `*.consigneeName`/`*.contactName` path is added, matching the Customer-slice precedent. Consignee/contact PII never rides in a URL query string (the anti-pattern the invoice-customer filter already avoids) and never in logs or spans.

7. **The GPS pipeline is untouched; capture still begins at Start.** The ADR-0035 c5 ingest predicate accepts pings only for an `IN_PROGRESS` trip (or a `COMPLETED` trip inside its window); the new pre-`IN_PROGRESS` states (`OFFERED`/`ACCEPTED`) are therefore **403 for ingest** — correct, because capture must start when the driver Starts, not when they Accept. No telematics code changes. The one real code break is the driver app's `isStartable`, which moves from `PLANNED` to `ACCEPTED` (c8); `isStoppable` (keys off `IN_PROGRESS`) is unaffected, and the trip-start SLI (counts `→ IN_PROGRESS` PATCHes) is unaffected. Lifetime stats (COMPLETED-only, `startedAt not null`) naturally exclude the new pre-start states.

8. **The driver app gains a request → accept → progress flow.** A third tab (**Requests**) lists the driver's `OFFERED` trips; **Accept** PATCHes `OFFERED → ACCEPTED` (stamping `acceptedAt`). The accepted trip appears in the Trips tab as **startable**. An **order-detail view** shows material, pickup/drop-off (as pins on a map + a **Navigate deep-link** to the device maps app for turn-by-turn), the consignee (tap-to-call), expected load count, special instructions, and docket field. While `IN_PROGRESS`, **live progress taps** — *Arrived at pickup / Loaded / Arrived at drop-off / Delivered* — PATCH the corresponding milestone timestamps (no status change; monotonic, non-decreasing). Everything rides the existing cookie-session, own-trip-scoped PATCH path (`DriverScopeService`); a driver still **cannot create or delete** trips (`trips:*` create/delete stay ADMIN/OFFICE_STAFF) — "orders come only from the admin app" holds by construction.

9. **Maps, navigation, and routing.** The admin dispatch UI reuses the sanctioned **Leaflet + react-leaflet** stack (the `/map` live view and the `geofences` pin/draw editor are the templates) with OSM tiles — pickup/drop-off are placed by clicking a marker or picking a saved Site. **The admin can pin a location and save it with a name (PO): that IS the `Site` aggregate (c4)** — `/sites/new` drops a pin on the map, names it, and it becomes a reusable pickup/drop-off option. The driver app has **no map today**; it adds **MapLibre-RN** (OSM raster, **no API key**, matching the web's tiles) behind the custom dev build, showing the pickup/drop-off pins and — while running — the driver's own position.

   **Turn-by-turn navigation is a maps deep-link (the real "Google Maps features", PO), and it ships first.** The driver's **Navigate** button opens the device's Google Maps (a `google.navigation:q=<lat>,<lng>` / `geo:` / `https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?...` URI) — which supplies **live-traffic routing, ETA, and voice turn-by-turn for free**, with no API key, no per-request cost, and no map dependency. This is exactly the Google-Maps experience the PO asked for, without FleetCo reimplementing a navigation engine, and it is available before the inline-map slice lands.

   **In-app route line + ETA come from a provider-agnostic `RoutingProvider` seam** (the Mailer-over-`resend` / LLM-client-over-DeepSeek pattern). A thin interface — `route(origin, destination) → { geometryLatLng, distanceMeters, durationSeconds }` — with a **no-network Mock** for dev/test/CI (so the build stays green with no external call) and a pluggable, **env-gated** implementation. So the admin dispatch map + trip detail (and the driver's order view) can **draw the route polyline and show an estimated "~45 min · 32 km" label** without leaving the app. The provider is chosen at activation, not hard-wired: **Google Directions/Routes API** gives the closest match to "Google Maps features" (live-traffic ETA) at the cost of an operator API key + per-request billing + coordinate egress to Google; a **free OSM router** (self-hosted OSRM, or OpenRouteService's free tier) gives a free-flow ETA with no key and no per-call cost. **Recommendation:** ship the Mock + seam now, default the live provider to **Google Directions** for the requested experience, and let the operator flip it at M1 via `ROUTING_PROVIDER` + the key — exactly how `RESEND_API_KEY` / `DEEPSEEK_API_KEY` gate their features. The in-app route+ETA is a **preview/estimate**; the driver's authoritative turn-by-turn is always the deep-link. Coordinate egress to the routing provider is recorded in the ADR-0013 amendment and, like every prior external egress, **gated on M1**.

10. **RBAC is the existing spine.** Dispatch (create/assign/order) and `Site` CRUD = ADMIN + OFFICE_STAFF (`trips:*` create already held by both; new `sites:*`). Accept + progress + start + complete = the driver PATCHing their **own** trip (existing own-record scope). No new capability is needed on the driver side; the route-class gate (`trips:*`) + the service-layer own-record predicate remain the two required walls.

11. **Explicitly deferred, to keep this lean.** **Not** in scope: `Trip.jobId` and trip-level pricing (their own ADR-0003-touching tech-debt items — the order attaches per-dispatch, not via a Job); the standing vehicle↔driver assignment (distinct deferred item); weighbridge tare/gross/net + OCR ticket capture; signature proof-of-delivery; per-load rates/accessorials/invoicing linkage; geofence-based automatic arrival detection (the deferred `Site.geofenceId`); marketplace auto-matching/offer-cascade; and the rest of Phase 5 (manager mobile, customer portal, route planning). The **delivered-quantity/POD-photo** option the PO did not take is likewise deferred; the docket field is a plain text stand-in.

12. **Sequencing: production use gates on M1; the build parallelizes.** Nine tickets, each its own PR, PO-ratified by merge, never self-merged: **W1** this ADR + the ADR-0003/0013 annotations + DESIGN.md §Trip-dispatch/§Sites (governance + design, no code) · **W2** schema + hand-authored migration (Site, MaterialType, Trip order columns + timestamps + OFFERED/ACCEPTED) + redact paths + round-trip test · **W3** the Sites CRUD module + `sites:*` · **W4** the Trip dispatch API (transition matrix, order validation, projections, driver-scope accept/progress; mutation-tested) · **W5** the admin Sites surface (Leaflet pin-and-name editor) · **W6** the admin dispatch UI + acceptance/progress timeline + live tracking + the `RoutingProvider` seam (Mock impl + a `route-preview` endpoint) drawing the route polyline + ETA · **W7** the driver Requests tab + Accept + order-detail view + Navigate deep-link (Google Maps turn-by-turn) · **W8** the driver inline map (route + ETA preview) + live progress taps · **W9** docs/memory close-out. W2–W9 are fully testable pre-deploy on the emulator against the dev API; nothing here blocks or displaces the deploy work, and real daily use waits on M1 like every prior exception.

## Alternatives considered

**Put the order on a `Dispatch` child aggregate (1:1 with Trip).** Cleaner separation, keeps the central Trip lean, better if the acceptance lifecycle grows (reject reasons, re-offer, timeouts). Rejected for v1 because the lifecycle is deliberately minimal (accept-only) and dispatch:trip is cardinality-1 — the ADR-0036 precedent (columns-on-Trip for cardinality-one data) applies directly, and a child aggregate would add a join and a second module for no v1 benefit. If the acceptance lifecycle later grows, extracting a `Dispatch` child is the named revisit.

**Put the order on `Job` and build `Trip.jobId` now.** Architecturally the "contract level" home (material + endpoints are stable across a two-week job's many loads), and it would finally wire the ADR-0003 spine. Rejected for v1 because `Trip.jobId` is its own ADR-0003-touching decision (a standing tech-debt item, deliberately deferred by ADR-0039), the PO's request is framed per-trip, and forcing a Job for every dispatch adds a step to the common "just send a truck" case. The order attaches per-dispatch on Trip; hoisting stable defaults to Job (once `Trip.jobId` exists) is a clean later enhancement, not a v1 requirement.

**Free-text pickup/drop-off (like `Customer.address` / `Job.description`).** Fastest, zero modelling. Rejected: the PO explicitly asked for **map pins like ride-sharing apps**, and a single company hauling the same crushers→sites would re-type identical lanes every trip, with no map, no distance, no arrival detection. The reusable `Site` aggregate is the right grain.

**Reuse `Geofence` as the pickup/drop-off location.** It is the existing reusable named-place entity. Rejected: it models a **polygon boundary**, not a **point**; its taxonomy has no crusher/pit type; and `CUSTOMER_SITE` fences are customer-owned (a crusher has no such owner). Overloading its taxonomy/ownership would corrupt the geofence-status machinery. `Site` optionally *links* to a Geofence later (c4) — the clean separation.

**A marketplace offer model (auto-match to a ranked driver pool, offer-cascade on decline/timeout).** The Uber pattern. Rejected: FleetCo is one company with employee drivers, not a marketplace — the dispatcher *chooses* the driver. Auto-matching, bidding, and offer-cascade are exactly the over-engineering the research flagged.

**A fine-grained status enum for every micro-stage** (`EN_ROUTE_TO_PICKUP`, `LOADING`, `EN_ROUTE_TO_DROPOFF`, `UNLOADING`, …). Rejected: mature dispatch systems track these as **stop-level timestamps within one in-progress order**, not as top-level statuses — an enum explosion complicates the transition matrix, every list filter, and every consumer for no gain. Timestamps it is (c1).

**Defer the whole program behind the first production deploy.** The deploy-first record's recommendation (ADR-0041's purpose; the analogous standing-assignment item was deferred two weeks ago). Surfaced to the PO and **declined**: this reshapes the central Trip contract, which is far cheaper to change before production trip data exists than after a migration of live trips, and the deploy is operator-gated (not agent-gated), so "wait for the deploy" is an indefinite wait for a program whose whole value is pre-data. The PO chose to build now via the c4 mechanism, production use M1-gated.

## Consequences

**Easier.** FleetCo can finally represent what it exists to run — the order a trip executes — so the driver knows where to go and what to haul, the office sees acceptance and live progress, and the central aggregate stops lying about being a contract executor while carrying no contract. The reusable `Site` pins set up map, distance, and future geofence arrival-detection for free. The change rides the existing PATCH-transition machinery, the own-record scope, the Leaflet stack, and the hybrid-Point pattern — mostly extension, little new mechanism.

**Harder / costs accepted.**

- **Maps in two apps** are the heaviest new surface. Mitigated: the web reuses the shipped Leaflet stack; the mobile map is one no-API-key dependency (MapLibre-RN) behind the custom dev build, and the Navigate deep-link ships first so navigation works before the inline map. Still, the mobile map is real new native surface with its own build/test cost.
- **A routing provider is a new external egress + recurring cost** (if Google Directions is the activated impl). Mitigated by the seam: dev/test/CI use the Mock (no call, no key, green build), the driver's authoritative turn-by-turn is the free Google Maps deep-link, and the paid in-app route-preview is an *estimate* the operator activates at M1 by choosing `ROUTING_PROVIDER` (Google for live-traffic ETA and its per-request cost, or a free OSM router for a free-flow ETA). Coordinate egress to the provider is recorded in the ADR-0013 amendment.
- **The central aggregate grows** — ~13 new Trip columns + 2 statuses + a new aggregate + a new enum. Every `.strict()` schema, the five-place status mirror, and the DI graph must move in lock-step; the full API suite (not changed-files) must run before each PR (the driver-app DI-ripple lesson).
- **Built against a system that cannot dispatch a real trip until M1.** Like every prior exception, this widens the built-but-undeployed surface the deploy-first gate worries about; accepted because the Trip-model reshape is cheapest pre-data and the deploy is unblocked in parallel.
- **New Tier-2 PII** (consignee + site contacts) must be redacted correctly and kept out of URLs/logs — one more place to get PII handling exactly right.
- **A ~9-slice program** is a large commitment ahead of the deploy; sequenced so value lands incrementally (admin dispatch + driver accept usable after W2–W7, before the inline map).

## Revisit when

- **The first production deploy reaches daily use** → dispatch becomes normal Phase-5 sequencing (ADR-0041's window closes on its own terms); re-estimate against real operator usage.
- **The acceptance lifecycle needs to grow** (decline-with-reason, re-offer, timeouts, an offer-to-a-second-driver) → extract the order + lifecycle into a `Dispatch` child aggregate (the rejected-for-v1 alternative).
- **`Trip.jobId` is wanted** (a job's trips enumerable; per-job P&L; hoisting order defaults to the Job) → the deferred ADR-0003-touching decision, taken in its own ADR; it composes cleanly with this order model.
- **Automatic arrival detection is wanted** → add the `Site.geofenceId` link and reuse the ADR-0030 geofence-status machinery.
- **The routing estimate proves insufficient** (a free-flow ETA drifts too far from reality, or live-traffic is wanted and the OSM router was chosen) → switch the `RoutingProvider` impl (the seam makes this an env/config change), or adopt Google's Routes API traffic-aware mode; if in-app navigation (not a deep-link) is ever wanted, that is a separate, heavier decision (an embedded navigation SDK, its own ADR).
- **Proof-of-delivery / weighbridge / pricing** become real needs → delivered-quantity + POD photo (the option not taken here), then scale-ticket capture, then per-load rates — each its own slice.
- **The material list needs per-row attributes** (density, default rate) → graduate `MaterialType` from an enum to a managed aggregate.

## Phase mismatch (ADR-0041 commitment 4, exercised — the fifth time)

This is new pull-forward feature scope opened before the first deploy reaches daily use — the fifth exercise of ADR-0041 commitment 4 (after ADR-0042, ADR-0043, ADR-0044/0045, and ADR-0046). The PO chose to open it with the deploy-first recommendation surfaced and **declined**, as with the prior four; this section is the fresh argument commitment 4 requires. The case, argued afresh: (a) this **completes the central Trip aggregate** ADR-0003 always intended as a contract-executor — a reshape ADR-0003 explicitly reserved the right to make — rather than adding a peripheral feature; (b) that reshape is **materially cheaper before production trip data exists** than as a migration of live trips afterward, so doing it pre-deploy is the *lower-risk* ordering for this specific change, not merely an impatience; (c) it is **fully synthetic/emulator-testable** and does not block or displace the operator-led deploy or the in-flight ADR-0035 D5/D6 stream; and (d) **all production use gates on M1** — no trip is really dispatched until the app runs in production. ADR-0025 is not cited as precedent. The near-identical *standing vehicle↔driver assignment* item stays deferred and distinct (a persistent pairing, not per-journey dispatch).

## Relationship to prior ADRs

- **Extends — does not supersede — ADR-0003 (Trip as central aggregate).** The Trip stays the central aggregate and the executor of a Job's work; this ADR gives it the dispatch lifecycle and the structured order ADR-0003 anticipated (*"the first place we will ADR-revise"*). ADR-0003's Trip-as-central decision, its `TripStarted/Ended/Cancelled` integration surface (now joined by dispatch/accept/progress events), and its "not everything is a Trip" boundary all stand.
- **Amends ADR-0013 (data classification)** with the consignee + site-contact Tier-2 PII fields and their redact paths (commitment 6) — the Customer-slice precedent, no new tier — and records the new **coordinate egress** to the routing provider (commitment 9), a data-flow of the same category as the ADR-0043 DeepSeek and ADR-0042 tile egress, gated on M1.
- **Reuses ADR-0029 (telematics storage)** hybrid-Point pattern for `Site`; **reuses ADR-0042 (live map)** Leaflet stack and `/map` for the admin pin picker + live tracking; **leaves ADR-0035 (driver-app GPS) untouched** (capture still starts at `IN_PROGRESS`; `isStartable` moves to `ACCEPTED`). The optional future `Site.geofenceId` reuses **ADR-0030 (geofences)**.
- **Does not build `Trip.jobId`** (the deferred ADR-0003-touching tech-debt item) nor the **standing vehicle↔driver assignment** (a distinct deferred item) — both remain owed and are explicitly out of scope. **ADR-0036 (engine-hours)** meter-reading rules are unchanged (its columns-on-Trip precedent is the model for the order columns).
- **Governed by ADR-0041 (deploy-first gate)** via its commitment-4 mechanism (§Phase mismatch); **ADR-0025 is not cited** as precedent.
