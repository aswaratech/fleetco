# ADR-0036: Engine-hours (hour-meter) tracking for the earthmoving fleet — a nullable hours dimension on the central Vehicle/Trip aggregate that mirrors the odometer machinery (a meter-type discriminator, integer tenths-of-an-hour readings, the Trip→Vehicle bump inside the existing transaction, and lifetime-hours stats)

- **Status:** Proposed
- **Date:** 2026-06-18
- **Decider:** Product owner (CEO)

## Context

FleetCo's fleet is two halves. One half rolls: trucks and tippers, whose wear, service, utilization, and resale all track **distance** — kilometers — which the system already measures through the odometer machinery built across iters 1–13. The other half **does not roll the way a truck does**: excavators, loaders, graders (the `VehicleKind` enum already names `EXCAVATOR`, `LOADER`, `GRADER`), plus the generators and cranes a heavy-construction company runs. For that earthmoving half, **kilometers are close to meaningless.** An excavator parked on a site digging for ten hours travels a few hundred meters; its odometer barely moves while its engine — and every wear-bearing component on it — runs hard. The number that matters for that machine is **engine-hours** (the hour-meter reading, the "Service Meter Reading" / SMR every operator and every manufacturer's service schedule speaks in).

The consequence is concrete and current: **on the equipment it owns, FleetCo is blind.** The four things the business needs from an asset are all hour-based for that half of the fleet, and none of them are expressible today:

- **Utilization** — "how hard is this excavator working?" is hours-run, not km-driven.
- **Preventive-maintenance triggers** — heavy equipment is serviced on hour intervals (e.g. a 250-engine-hour oil/filter service), which has **no relation** to the 5,000–10,000 km interval a truck's oil change follows. A PM aggregate keyed only on km (or calendar) cannot schedule equipment service at all. (This is the prerequisite for the maintenance-scheduling decision queued next as DEC2/ADR-0037, which depends on the engine-hours trigger this ADR provides.)
- **Rental billing** — when equipment is hired out (or costed against a job), the unit is engine-hours, not distance.
- **Resale valuation** — a used excavator is priced on its hour-meter, the way a used truck is priced on its odometer.

The odometer machinery this ADR rotates is worth stating precisely, because the recommendation is "the odometer, rotated" and a future reader should see exactly what is being mirrored. It is, today (verified against `apps/api/prisma/schema.prisma` and `apps/api/src/modules/trips/trips.service.ts`):

- **`Vehicle.odometerStartKm Int @default(0)`** — the baseline odometer at acquisition; and **`Vehicle.odometerCurrentKm Int @default(0)`** — the vehicle's current reading, which `VehiclesService.create` defaults to `odometerStartKm` ("current at acquisition equals start").
- **`Trip.startOdometerKm Int?`** and **`Trip.endOdometerKm Int?`** — nullable readings captured by the status transitions: `→IN_PROGRESS` records `startedAt` + `startOdometerKm`, `→COMPLETED` records `endedAt` + `endOdometerKm`.
- **The Trip→Vehicle odometer auto-update** (iter 11): when a Trip *transitions* into `COMPLETED`, `TripsService.update` advances the referenced `Vehicle.odometerCurrentKm` to the trip's `endOdometerKm` — **but only when `endOdometerKm > vehicle.odometerCurrentKm`** (the `>` avoids a no-op and prevents a backdated correction trip from moving the odometer backward; "once forward, stays forward"). The trip write and the vehicle write run inside **one Prisma interactive `$transaction`** so a mid-flight failure cannot leave the trip `COMPLETED` with a stale vehicle reading.
- **Per-vehicle lifetime stats** (`statsForVehicle`, iter 12) and the symmetric **per-driver stats** (`statsForDriver`, iter 13): three aggregations in one `$transaction` — `completedTripCount`, `totalKmLogged` (sum of `endOdometerKm − startOdometerKm` across `COMPLETED` trips), and `mostRecentDriver`/`mostRecentVehicle`.
- **The driver-app trip-stop screen** (ADR-0034 D2; `apps-mobile/driver/src/trips.ts`): `tripStopPayload(endOdometerKm, nowIso)` is the mobile capture point — a driver completing a trip on the phone enters the end odometer, and the server bumps the vehicle. This is a live Phase-2 surface, so the hours dimension is a cross-surface change, not a backend-only one.

Two existing precedents make the storage choice cheap to settle:

- **Integer minor units extend from money to quantity.** CLAUDE.md mandates money as integer minor units (paisa) and forbids floats; the Fuel-logs slice (iter 19) **mechanically extended that rule to a physical quantity** — `FuelLog.litersMl` stores liters as integer milliliters precisely because "the same precision-loss argument CLAUDE.md makes for money applies to fuel quantity." Engine-hours are a physical quantity read to a tenth of an hour; the same rule applies, and `litersMl` is the precedent to copy rather than inventing a `Float`/`Decimal` path.
- **Nullable-but-present columns on the central aggregate are an established move.** The iter-14 compliance metadata added eight nullable columns to `Vehicle` "because the aggregate shipped since iter 1 without these columns; existing rows must remain readable." The hours dimension follows the same posture: nullable, additive, backward-compatible.

This ADR is required because the change touches the **central domain aggregate**. `Trip` is FleetCo's central aggregate (ADR-0003), and `Vehicle` is the foundation aggregate every downstream slice references by id. A schema change to `Vehicle` **and** `Trip`, plus a new branch in the trip-completion transaction and the driver-app trip-stop surface, is exactly the "touches the database schema / a cross-cutting concern / the central aggregate" change CLAUDE.md step 4 says is PO-confirmed before code. Per the project's established pattern (ADR-0029 settled telematics storage before the GPS code; ADR-0034 settled mobile auth before the auth code), the confirmation vehicle is an ADR. **This ADR decides shape and policy only. It writes no code, no `schema.prisma` edit, no migration, and adds no dependency.** Those land with the implementing slice (Program B's engine-hours ticket) once this ADR is accepted.

### Phase mismatch (stated honestly)

This is not Phase-2 scope. The equipment/earthmoving half of the fleet — utilization, hour-based PM, rental — is **Phase-3 headline material** (the roadmap's "Program B"). Two facts must be on the record so the PO weighs the pull-forward with eyes open, not as a fait accompli:

1. **This proposes Phase-3 work ahead of its phase.** It does so under the **ADR-0025 ahead-of-gate pattern** — the same documented-exception mechanism that opened Phase 2 before Phase 1 reached daily use. But ADR-0025 was explicitly a *one-time* exception scoped to the Phase-1 → Phase-2 transition; pulling Phase-3 schema forward is a **new** exception that needs its own deliberate decision. This ADR is where that decision is recorded as a *proposal*; accepting it is what authorizes the pull-forward.
2. **The first production deploy is still owed.** Phase 1 is content-complete but **not in daily use** — the deploy, the restore-from-backup drill, live SLI/DORA reporting, and the retrospective sign-off all remain open (`docs/CURRENT_PHASE.md`; ADR-0025 commitment 1). Every schema dimension added before the deploy widens the "built-but-unused" surface ADR-0025 flagged as its central risk: code shipped against assumption, never exercised by a real operator. Adding a Phase-3 dimension to the central aggregate while the app has never run in production is a real cost, not a free option.

The honest framing: **this ADR proposes the pull-forward; it does not assume it.** The PO decides at acceptance whether to (a) accept and let Program B build the engine-hours slice now, (b) accept but hold the implementing slice until the deploy lands or Phase 3 opens, or (c) reject the pull-forward and keep this as a Proposed record for when Phase 3 arrives. An agent must never self-accept this ADR, add an `## Acceptance` block, or flip the status — the PO ratifies separately.

## Decision

**Add an engine-hours (hour-meter) dimension to the central `Vehicle`/`Trip` aggregate by rotating the existing odometer machinery: a `meterType` discriminator on `Vehicle` (recommended values `ODOMETER_KM` [default], `ENGINE_HOURS`, `BOTH`) that says which meter(s) an asset has; nullable integer engine-hours columns on `Vehicle` (start + current) and `Trip` (start + end), stored as integer minor units — tenths of an hour, never a float — mirroring `odometerStartKm`/`odometerCurrentKm` and `startOdometerKm`/`endOdometerKm`; a Trip→Vehicle hours auto-update that runs inside the *same* `COMPLETED`-transition `$transaction` as the existing odometer bump, with the identical monotonic "once forward, stays forward" rule; per-asset lifetime-hours stats (`totalHoursLogged`) added alongside `totalKmLogged`; and a meter-aware trip-stop capture that prompts for the reading(s) the vehicle's `meterType` calls for, on both the web edit form and the ADR-0034 driver-app trip-stop screen.** Eight commitments define the shape, grouped. As with its sibling decision-records, this ADR recommends and leaves the genuinely owner-level picks — above all the storage shape and the exact precision scale — to the PO at acceptance.

### A. The meter dimension — what kind of meter an asset has

1. **Add a `meterType` enum + column on `Vehicle`, defaulting to `ODOMETER_KM`.** Recommended values: **`ODOMETER_KM`** (trucks, tippers — the distance-metered half), **`ENGINE_HOURS`** (excavators, loaders, graders, generators, cranes — the hour-metered half), and **`BOTH`** (the occasional asset where both matter — e.g. a truck-mounted crane whose chassis runs on km but whose boom runs on hours). The default is **`ODOMETER_KM`** so the migration is backward-compatible: every existing row keeps its current km-only behavior with no data change, and the operator flips equipment to `ENGINE_HOURS` (or `BOTH`) as they classify the fleet. The discriminator is what makes the rest of the system unambiguous — which reading the trip-stop UI prompts for, which stat the detail page shows, which meter a future PM schedule keys on — **independent of whether a reading has been captured yet** (a brand-new excavator with no SMR recorded is still an `ENGINE_HOURS` asset). This is the same reason the compliance columns are nullable-but-present: "tracks hours" and "has an hours reading yet" are different facts and must not be conflated. An explicit column is preferred over deriving the meter from `vehicle.kind` (see Alternatives) because `kind` is a chassis taxonomy, `OTHER` assets can be either, and overloading `kind` with a metering policy is the kind of implicit coupling that breaks quietly.

### B. The hours readings — the odometer, rotated

2. **Nullable engine-hours columns on `Vehicle`, mirroring the odometer pair.** Add an engine-hours **start** (baseline at acquisition) and **current** reading, the rotation of `odometerStartKm`/`odometerCurrentKm`. Unlike the odometer columns (which are `Int @default(0)`), these are **nullable** — `null` is the honest value for a km-only asset (it has no hour-meter) and for an hour-metered asset registered before its SMR has been keyed in. The conceptual names the kickoff uses are `engineHoursStart`/`engineHoursCurrent`; the concrete column names carry the stored unit per house convention (see commitment 4), and the create-path default mirrors the odometer's ("current at acquisition equals start" — when an `ENGINE_HOURS`/`BOTH` asset is created with a start reading and no current, current defaults to start).

3. **Nullable engine-hours columns on `Trip`, captured at the same transitions.** Add a trip **start** and **end** engine-hours reading, the rotation of `startOdometerKm`/`endOdometerKm` — nullable, captured by the exact same status transitions: `→IN_PROGRESS` records the start hours alongside `startedAt`, `→COMPLETED` records the end hours alongside `endedAt`. For an `ENGINE_HOURS` asset, the trip captures hours and not km; for `ODOMETER_KM`, km and not hours; for `BOTH`, both (see commitment 7 for the meter-aware validation).

4. **Precision: integer minor units — tenths of an hour — never a float.** Engine-hours read to a tenth of an hour (an hour-meter shows e.g. `1234.5`). Per CLAUDE.md's never-floats rule for money, **extended to physical quantity by the `FuelLog.litersMl` precedent**, the readings are stored as **integer tenths of an hour** (so `1234.5 h` stores as `12345`), and the column names encode the unit exactly as `litersMl`/`odometerCurrentKm`/`amountPaisa` do. Display divides by ten; cross-field math stays in integers. **The single genuinely open pick this ADR leaves to acceptance is the exact scale** — tenths (deci-hours, matching the hour-meter's native 0.1 h resolution; recommended) versus hundredths (centi-hours, headroom for sub-tenth telemetry feeds later) versus a Prisma `Decimal` (rejected here for the same precision-and-consistency reasons `litersMl` rejected it, but the PO's to override). The load-bearing rule — **integer minor units, not a float** — is fixed; the scale is the dial.

### C. The auto-update and the stats — reuse the proven machinery

5. **The Trip→Vehicle hours auto-update runs inside the existing `COMPLETED`-transition transaction.** When a Trip transitions into `COMPLETED`, the same `$transaction` that already bumps `Vehicle.odometerCurrentKm` **also** advances `Vehicle.engineHoursCurrent` to the trip's end-hours reading — under the **identical monotonic rule**: only when the trip's end hours are strictly greater than the vehicle's current hours (no no-op write; no backward movement from a backdated correction trip; "once forward, stays forward"). One transaction, now bumping up to two meters, so a mid-flight failure can never leave the trip `COMPLETED` with a stale vehicle on either dimension. The compensating action for a wrong reading is identical to the odometer's: an operator edits the vehicle's current hours on the edit form (the documented manual correction path).

6. **Per-asset lifetime-hours stats mirror the lifetime-km stats.** Extend `statsForVehicle` (and the symmetric `statsForDriver`) with **`totalHoursLogged`** — the sum of `endEngineHours − startEngineHours` across `COMPLETED` trips, the rotation of `totalKmLogged` — computed in the same `$transaction` snapshot. The Vehicle detail page's "Lifetime stats" section shows km, hours, or both per the asset's `meterType` (an `ENGINE_HOURS` excavator shows hours where a truck shows km).

### D. Cross-surface capture and validation

7. **Meter-aware capture and cross-field validation, on both the web and driver-app trip surfaces.** The end-≥-start cross-field rule that guards the odometer is rotated for hours (a trip cannot end with fewer engine-hours than it started). The trip-stop capture **branches on the vehicle's `meterType`**: an `ODOMETER_KM` asset prompts for the end odometer (unchanged); an `ENGINE_HOURS` asset prompts for the end hours; a `BOTH` asset prompts for both. This is the **cross-surface** reach of the change — it touches the web trip create/edit forms **and** the ADR-0034 driver-app trip-stop screen (`apps-mobile/driver/src/trips.ts`'s `tripStopPayload`, which today takes only `endOdometerKm` and would grow an hours-aware variant). The standalone mobile app cannot import the API's types, so its slim payload shapes grow in lockstep — a real, if mechanical, second surface.

### E. Scope boundaries — what this ADR does not decide

8. **Out of scope, stated so the boundaries are clean.** This ADR does **not** decide: (a) **automated hour-meter reads** from telematics/CAN-bus — readings are **operator-entered** at trip stop for now, exactly as odometer readings are; an automated feed (the GPS/telematics stack is the natural future source) is a later revisit, and the integer-tenths storage is chosen partly to receive it cleanly; (b) the **preventive-maintenance scheduling** that consumes the engine-hours trigger — that is DEC2/ADR-0037, which depends on this ADR but is its own decision; (c) **rental-billing or utilization reporting** built on hours — future Program-B/Phase-3 features that read these columns; (d) **retroactive backfill UX** for keying in historical hour-meter readings on existing equipment — an implementing-slice detail; and (e) any change to the **km** path — odometer behavior is untouched and fully backward-compatible.

### Relationship to prior ADRs (what this builds on, consumes, and unlocks)

- **Builds on ADR-0003 (Trip as the central aggregate)** — the reason the hours dimension lives *on* `Vehicle`/`Trip` rather than in a new `Asset` aggregate: an excavator is a fleet asset that takes fuel, incurs expenses, runs trips (a dig-job is a trip), and is referenced by `vehicleId` throughout; forking it out would fracture the aggregate every downstream slice is built on (see Alternatives).
- **Builds on the iter-11/12/13 odometer machinery** (the `COMPLETED`-transition `$transaction`, the monotonic bump, `statsForVehicle`/`statsForDriver`) and **the iter-14 nullable-compliance-columns precedent** (nullable-but-present additive columns on the central aggregate).
- **Consumes the `FuelLog.litersMl` precedent** — integer-minor-units-for-quantity, the answer to "how do we store a fractional reading without a float."
- **Touches ADR-0034's driver-app trip-stop screen** as a live consumer surface (commitment 7), so the implementing slice coordinates the web and mobile capture together.
- **Invokes the ADR-0025 ahead-of-gate pattern** for the phase pull-forward (see "Phase mismatch"), as a new, separately-decided exception — not as a free extension of the Phase-1 → Phase-2 one.
- **Unlocks DEC2/ADR-0037 (preventive-maintenance & service-scheduling)**, which needs the engine-hours trigger this provides, and feeds future utilization and rental-billing features.
- **Is the PO-confirmation vehicle** for the central-aggregate schema change per CLAUDE.md step 4 — no engine-hours code is written until this ADR is accepted.

## Alternatives considered

**A separate `Asset` aggregate for hour-metered equipment.** Split excavators/loaders/graders out of `Vehicle` into their own table/aggregate metered in hours, leaving `Vehicle` for km-rolling trucks. Rejected as the heaviest possible change for a dimension two nullable columns model cleanly. It would fracture the central aggregate (ADR-0003): a `Trip` would need a polymorphic FK to either `Vehicle` or `Asset` (or two nullable FKs), and **every** downstream aggregate that keys off `vehicleId` — fuel logs, expense logs, GPS pings, geofences, the per-vehicle cost and efficiency reports — would have to double its query surface or grow a parallel asset path. An excavator genuinely *is* a fleet asset that takes fuel, incurs expenses, and runs trips; it belongs in `Vehicle`, just metered differently. The cost of the split is enormous and permanent; the benefit (a "cleaner" taxonomy) is cosmetic.

**A `meterType` discriminator only, with no hours columns.** Add the enum now, defer the readings. Rejected as a non-decision — the flag without the columns stores nothing and schedules no work; it is "half the feature" dressed as a decision. The discriminator and the columns are one change.

**Parallel `*Hours` columns with no `meterType` discriminator** (rely on nullability alone to mean "this is an hours asset"). Rejected as the *recommendation* because it cannot distinguish an hour-metered asset that simply has no reading yet (`null` because un-keyed) from a km asset that will never have one (`null` because not applicable) — the trip-stop UI would have to guess, or fall back to deriving the meter from `vehicle.kind`. Kept available as the lighter option the PO may prefer if they accept that ambiguity and a `kind`-based heuristic; the recommendation is the explicit discriminator.

**Derive the meter from `vehicle.kind`** (`EXCAVATOR`/`LOADER`/`GRADER` ⇒ hours; `TRUCK`/`TIPPER` ⇒ km). Rejected: it overloads `kind` (a chassis-type taxonomy with an `OTHER` escape hatch) with a metering policy, breaks for `OTHER` assets (which can be either), cannot express `BOTH`, and couples two concerns that drift independently. An explicit `meterType` is one column and unambiguous; deriving it is clever and brittle.

**Store hours as a `Float` or Prisma `Decimal`.** Rejected for `Float` outright per CLAUDE.md's never-floats rule (the precision-loss argument that the `litersMl` slice already extended from money to quantity). `Decimal` is the closer call — it is exact — but it is heavier, invites mixed decimal/float arithmetic at call sites, and would be the *only* fractional-quantity column in a schema that has deliberately standardized on integer minor units (`paisa`, `litersMl`). The integer-tenths approach keeps one consistent convention; `Decimal` survives only as the PO's override.

**A generic polymorphic `meterReading` table** (one row per reading, any unit, any asset). Rejected as over-engineering — it is the entity-attribute-value anti-pattern applied to two fixed, well-known dimensions (km and hours). Two typed column-pairs are simpler, are indexable, mirror the proven odometer shape, and do not require a join to read a vehicle's current reading. A reading-history table is a legitimate *future* structure if per-reading audit trails are ever needed, but it is not warranted to store a current value.

**Build the engine-hours schema now without a separate ADR.** Rejected on CLAUDE.md step 4: a schema change to the central `Vehicle`/`Trip` aggregate, a new branch in the trip-completion transaction, and a cross-surface change reaching the driver app are precisely the changes that warrant PO confirmation before code — and this one additionally pulls Phase-3 scope forward, which is its own decision. The ADR is that gate, mirroring ADR-0029 (storage before GPS code) and ADR-0034 (auth shape before auth code).

## Consequences

### What this makes easier

The earthmoving half of the fleet stops being invisible. Equipment utilization, **hour-based preventive-maintenance triggers** (the direct unblock for DEC2/ADR-0037), hour-based rental billing, and hour-based resale valuation all become expressible against a real stored reading instead of living in the operator's head or a spreadsheet. Because the design is "the odometer, rotated," the implementing slice reuses proven machinery — the `COMPLETED`-transition `$transaction`, the monotonic bump, the stats aggregation — so the novelty (and the risk) is low. The change is fully backward-compatible: `meterType` defaults to `ODOMETER_KM`, the hours columns are nullable, and not one existing row or one line of the km path changes. And it is legible memory: a future reader sees one ADR explaining that FleetCo meters two ways, why hours live on the central aggregate, and why the readings are integer tenths.

### What this makes harder

The central aggregate grows a parallel dimension, and the cost is paid at every surface that touches a reading. The trip-completion transaction now reasons about up to two meters. The trip-stop capture — on the web edit form **and** the ADR-0034 driver-app trip-stop screen — must branch on `meterType` to prompt for the right reading(s), so the mobile app's slim payload shapes grow in lockstep with the API. The stats and the Vehicle detail page grow an hours variant. Cross-field validation grows an hours end-≥-start rule and a meter-consistency rule ("an `ENGINE_HOURS` asset's trip captures hours, not km"). Each of these is a standing "which meter?" branch that every future trip-touching change must keep honoring — the same kind of standing obligation the nullable-FK and Tier-marking conventions already impose.

### Costs we accept

- **A nullable column dimension on the two highest-traffic aggregates.** `Vehicle` and `Trip` carry columns that are `null` for most of today's rows (the km fleet). We accept this as the honest shape — the same trade the iter-14 compliance columns made — rather than forcing a `0`/sentinel that would lie about un-metered assets.
- **The `meterType` discriminator is a new invariant the trip lifecycle must honor.** A trip-stop flow that forgets to branch on it captures the wrong reading. We accept this as a bounded, code-reviewed surface, mitigated by cross-field validation.
- **Readings are operator-entered, not telemetry-fed.** Until an automated hour-meter source exists, the hours number is as reliable as the operator's data entry — exactly the current state of the odometer. We accept this; the integer-tenths storage is chosen partly so an automated feed slots in later without a schema change.
- **This is set without production telemetry and the deploy is still owed (ADR-0025).** The dimension is sized against assumption about how the operator will classify and read meters in the field, not against observed use. We accept this as the explicit, PO-decided cost of the phase pull-forward — and it is reversible in the sense that an *unaccepted* ADR ships nothing.

## Revisit when

- **PO acceptance fixes the open picks.** Acceptance ratifies the storage shape (the recommended `meterType` discriminator + nullable hours columns, versus columns-only, versus the rejected `Asset` aggregate) and the precision scale (tenths versus hundredths versus `Decimal`). If the PO chooses differently from the recommendations, the implementing slice follows the PO's pick and this ADR is annotated.
- **The PO rules on the phase pull-forward.** Acceptance is also where the PO decides whether the implementing slice proceeds now, waits for the production deploy / Phase 3, or is held entirely (see "Phase mismatch"). An undeployed Phase 1 carrying pulled-forward Phase-3 schema is the ADR-0025 "built-but-unused" risk; if it accumulates, that is the signal the deploy must precede further pull-forwards.
- **Telematics / CAN-bus can auto-read hour-meters.** The trigger to revisit operator-entry — the column becomes the sink for an automated feed, and the precision scale may need to widen.
- **An asset needs a third meter dimension** (operating cycles, liters pumped, drum-rotations). The trigger to revisit whether the typed-column-pair approach generalizes or whether a reading table is finally warranted.
- **`BOTH`-meter assets prove common — or vanishingly rare.** If common, revisit the trip-stop UX for dual capture; if effectively never used, revisit whether `BOTH` earns its place or whether the rare dual-meter asset is modeled some other way.
- **DEC2/ADR-0037 (preventive maintenance) is designed.** Confirm the engine-hours trigger shape this ADR provides matches what the PM scheduler needs; adjust at that ADR if a gap appears.
