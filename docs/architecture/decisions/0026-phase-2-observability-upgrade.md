# ADR-0026: Phase-2 observability upgrade — externally-hosted OTLP backend, traces-first then metrics, mandatory span scrubbing, two new SLIs

- **Status:** Proposed
- **Date:** 2026-06-03
- **Decider:** Product owner (CEO) — pending acceptance

## Context

Phase 2 ("Driver app and telematics") is open per ADR-0025. The roadmap names a specific operational prerequisite for it (`docs/product/roadmap.md` §"Phase 2 — Driver app and telematics"): "The observability stack is upgraded from logs-only to logs-plus-traces-plus-metrics, with one externally hosted backend chosen (candidates include SigNoz, Honeycomb, or Datadog free tier). Distributed tracing is in place across the driver app, the BullMQ jobs that process telematics events, and the R2 uploads for any captured media." This ADR is the decision that opens that work. It is one of three Phase-2 foundation ADRs proposed together — ADR-0027 (GPS data-classification amendment) and ADR-0028 (office-staff RBAC) are its siblings, and this ADR forward-references both where they bind on it.

The substrate this ADR turns on already exists. ADR-0024 wired OpenTelemetry into the API by **extending the global TracerProvider that Sentry v9 already owns** — Sentry auto-instruments HTTP, Prisma, and Redis; every log line carries `trace_id`/`span_id` via a pino mixin (`apps/api/src/app.module.ts`); and an **env-gated OTLP/HTTP span exporter** is built by `buildOtlpSpanProcessors(endpoint)` in `apps/api/src/observability/otel.ts`, dormant until `OTEL_EXPORTER_OTLP_ENDPOINT` is set. ADR-0024 deliberately scoped itself to the *substrate* and deferred exactly the four decisions this ADR makes — quoting its commitment 7 and "Revisit when": "Choosing and operating an externally-hosted tracing backend (SigNoz, Honeycomb, Datadog, Grafana Tempo, or a self-hosted OTel Collector) and adding OpenTelemetry *metrics* … are deferred to Phase 2," and "[the revisit names] the vendor or self-hosted topology, the sampling policy, and — load-bearing — the span-attribute scrubbing that the Tier 2/Tier 3 egress concern … requires before any span leaves the process." This ADR is the Phase-2 decision ADR-0024 pointed at. **Because the exporter already exists, turning traces on is fundamentally a configuration + vendor decision, not a re-architecture** — that framing shapes everything below.

The constraints that bound the choice are the ones every FleetCo infra decision lives inside. ADR-0014 fixes the production topology: a **single 2–4 GB VPS** running Postgres+PostGIS, Redis, the API, the web app, and Caddy under Docker Compose, operated by **one person** (the CEO, technically literate, not a hands-on coder), for a **single-company internal tool in Nepal where cost matters**. ADR-0011 sets the reliability model: two SLIs (API availability, trip-creation success) at a deliberately modest 99.0% over a rolling 28-day window, and its "Revisit when" already anticipates this moment — "Phase 2's driver app will likely need a 'telematics ping freshness' SLI." ADR-0013 sets the data-classification posture whose span-attribute analogue ADR-0024 flagged as owed. And ADR-0025 records that Phase 2 is being built **ahead of the production deploy**, so there is **no live production telemetry yet** — these decisions are made against assumptions, not measured load, and several targets below are explicitly provisional pending that data.

## Decision

**Upgrade Phase-2 observability from logs-only to logs+traces+metrics shipped to one externally-hosted, OTLP-native, *managed* backend (recommended: Grafana Cloud free tier; Honeycomb the close runner-up — final pick is the PO's), turned on traces-first through the env-gated exporter ADR-0024 already wired, with metrics following alongside the telematics slice; a default 100%-sampling policy with the high-frequency GPS-ping path the one sampling exception; a mandatory span-attribute scrub seam in the OTLP egress path that no span bypasses; and two new provisional SLIs (telematics-ping-freshness, driver-app-trip-start-success).** Seven commitments define the shape; this ADR writes **no code** — it fixes the decisions the implementing tickets build.

1. **One externally-hosted, managed, OTLP-native backend — not self-hosted.** The backend must (a) ingest OTLP/HTTP so the ADR-0024 exporter ships to it with no SDK swap, (b) be **managed/SaaS**, because self-hosting an observability stack on the single 2–4 GB VPS directly contradicts ADR-0014 — SigNoz's ClickHouse or a full self-hosted Grafana LGTM stack would compete with Postgres+PostGIS, Redis, the API, the web app, and Caddy for the same scarce RAM, turning a one-person ops box into two systems to run — and (c) ideally cover **all three signals (logs, traces, metrics) under one vendor**, since the roadmap's goal is one unified backend, not three. The **lead recommendation is Grafana Cloud's free tier**: it is the only candidate that natively stores all three signals (Loki/Tempo/Mimir), ingests OTLP, costs nothing at this scale, and adds zero ops to the VPS. **Honeycomb is the close runner-up** — best-in-class trace querying and a generous free tier, OTLP-native, zero ops — dispreferred only because it is a traces+wide-events+metrics product, not a logs store, so it would not unify the pino logs into the same pane. **The final pick is the PO's**, because it is a vendor relationship + credentials decision (a SaaS account, billing identity, and data-residency choice) that is properly the operator's, not the agent's. Exact free-tier quotas drift, so the implementing/turn-on ticket verifies current limits at signup rather than trusting a number frozen here.

2. **Self-hosted SigNoz and "Datadog free tier" are dispreferred, with reasons.** **Self-hosted SigNoz** is rejected on the ADR-0014 resource constraint above (its ClickHouse backend is heavier than the application it would observe). **"Datadog free tier"** — named in the roadmap — does **not** actually include APM/distributed tracing for free (the free tier is infra metrics with short retention and a small host cap; tracing is a paid product), so it cannot satisfy the "traces in an externally-hosted backend" goal without becoming a paid relationship, and Datadog is the most expensive option at scale — dispreferred on both counts. **SigNoz Cloud** (the managed offering, distinct from self-hosted SigNoz) remains a viable OTLP-native option and is not excluded; it simply ranks below Grafana Cloud and Honeycomb on free-tier clarity and vendor maturity for a one-person shop. A **self-hosted OTel Collector** is not a backend at all — it is an optional on-box *forwarding/scrubbing agent* in front of whichever SaaS is chosen (see commitment 5), and is lightweight enough for the VPS in that role only.

3. **Staged rollout: traces-first (now, config-only), then metrics (with telematics), then log-shipping (last).** The three signals do not land at once. **Traces first**, because the exporter already exists — turning it on is primarily setting `OTEL_EXPORTER_OTLP_ENDPOINT` and an auth header (see commitment 7), adding **no new dependency**. **Metrics second**, landing *alongside* the GPS-ping/BullMQ telematics slice, because metrics require new code and dependencies (an OpenTelemetry `MeterProvider` plus `@opentelemetry/sdk-metrics` and an OTLP metrics exporter) **and** because the headline Phase-2 metric — telematics-ping-freshness — has nothing to measure until pings exist; instrumenting metrics before the telematics aggregate ships would be instrumenting an empty room. **Log-shipping last and lowest-priority**, because logs already work (pino → stdout → Docker on the VPS) and are already trace-correlated via `trace_id`/`span_id` (ADR-0024); shipping them into the unified backend (via an OTLP logs exporter or a Collector/agent → Loki) is a convenience that buys a single query pane, not a missing capability, and it inherits the same scrub boundary as commitment 5 (the pino `redact` list and the span-scrub denylist must stay in sync — they are the same "no Tier 2/3 leaves the process" rule expressed at two layers).

4. **Sampling: ParentBased / 100% by default; the GPS-ping ingestion path is the one exception.** At single-company volume (one CEO today; tens of drivers in Phase 2, not millions of users) the cheapest correct policy is to **keep everything** — `ParentBased(AlwaysOn)`, head-based, 100% — so every trace is available for debugging and we stay far under any free-tier quota. The **one exception** is the high-frequency telematics-ping ingestion path: GPS pings arriving every few seconds across the fleet could individually overwhelm both the quota and the signal-to-noise of the trace view. The decision is that the **unit of tracing on that path is the BullMQ batch-processing job, not the individual ping** (a ping is a data record, not inherently a span); if per-ping spans are ever needed, they get `ParentBased(TraceIdRatioBased)` ratio sampling, not 100%. One technical consequence of ADR-0024's "extend Sentry's single TracerProvider" architecture: **the sampler is shared** — Sentry's recording and the OTLP export ride the same provider, so the sampling decision is made once (governed in Sentry v9 by `tracesSampleRate`/`tracesSampler` in `Sentry.init`) and both destinations see a consistent sampled set; you cannot sample differently for Sentry vs. the OTLP backend from the same provider. The implementing ticket pins the exact mechanism.

5. **Mandatory span-attribute scrubbing in the OTLP egress path — load-bearing, no span exempt.** This is the commitment ADR-0024 deferred and ADR-0013 requires. Before any span leaves the process, a **FleetCo-authored scrub seam** in `apps/api/src/observability/` (mirroring the existing pure-helper pattern in `otel.ts`) strips a **denylist** of sensitive attributes. The recommended mechanism is an OpenTelemetry **`SpanProcessor` (or exporter wrapper) positioned ahead of the OTLP `BatchSpanProcessor`** in the processor list — **not** Sentry's `beforeSendSpan` hook *alone*, because `beforeSendSpan` governs only what Sentry sends to Sentry and would leave the OTLP exporter's egress unscrubbed; if Sentry's own egress also needs scrubbing, a `beforeSendSpan` is added *in parallel*, but the OTLP path is the one this seam must cover. The denylist **mirrors and extends the pino `redact` paths** already in `app.module.ts` (`authorization`, `cookie`, `set-cookie`, `password`, `token`, `secret`, `email`, `driverName`, `licenseNumber`, `phoneNumber`, `contactPerson`), translated into span-attribute keys, across four categories:
   - **GPS coordinates / geometry** — any latitude/longitude/geofence attribute on telematics spans. **Their tier is set by ADR-0027 (sibling, proposed next in this program); this scrub list must honor whatever tier ADR-0027 assigns** and is finalized against it. The default posture until then: GPS coordinates never appear in an exported span attribute.
   - **PII embedded in URLs** — `http.url`, `http.target`, `url.full`, `url.query` can carry a query-string email or filter value, the exact hazard ADR-0024 named and the same reason the SLI signal in `app.module.ts` deliberately omits `req.url`.
   - **Driver / customer identifiers** — names, license/PAN numbers, phone, `contactPerson` — should any reach span attributes, including via Prisma's `db.statement` if it captures parameter *values* rather than the parameterized query (the implementing ticket verifies whether Sentry's `prismaIntegration` embeds values).
   - **Auth material** — `http.request.header.authorization`, `http.request.header.cookie`, any `*.token`/`*.secret` (Tier 1, never exported).

   The seam is the precondition for commitment 3: **traces do not export to any real backend until this scrub seam exists and is tested.**

6. **Two new SLIs at provisional targets; the existing two SLOs are unchanged here.** Per ADR-0011's "Revisit when" (which named the first of these), the SLI catalog expands by two:
   - **Telematics-ping-freshness** — the share, over the rolling 28-day window, of on-trip vehicles whose most-recent GPS ping is within a freshness threshold (provisionally ≤ 5 minutes). This is naturally a **metric** (a gauge per vehicle; the SLI is the fraction under threshold), which is part of why metrics land with the telematics slice (commitment 3). **Provisional target: 95.0%** — *deliberately lower than the 99.0% API SLO* because ping delivery depends on third-party mobile-network conditions in mountainous Nepal terrain (dead zones, tunnels, a phone parked in a basement); a missing ping from a vehicle in a coverage gap is not a FleetCo failure, and a 99% target would punish the system for the cellular network.
   - **Driver-app-trip-start-success** — the share of driver-app trip-start attempts that complete without a user-visible error; the Phase-2 analogue of the existing trip-creation-success SLI, computed the same way (a per-attempt `sli_good` signal). **Provisional target: 99.0%**, matching the existing core-operation SLO because a failed trip-start blocks a driver from working. **One genuine open definitional question** to settle when the driver app's offline/queueing behaviour is designed: whether app-side network failures (the app cannot reach the API) count against this SLI or whether it measures only server-side failures of reachable requests. That choice materially changes the number and is flagged here rather than silently assumed.

   Both targets are **provisional** in the same sense ADR-0011's original 99.0% was — to be calibrated against real data. ADR-0011 said the existing two SLOs would "tighten in Phase 2"; that tightening **needs the production data the deferred deploy will produce (ADR-0025) and is therefore not done here** — this ADR adds two SLIs and leaves ADR-0011's existing two SLOs exactly as set.

7. **Dependencies, the auth-header nuance, and who-sees-traces.** **Traces-first adds no new dependency** — the OTLP trace exporter, `BatchSpanProcessor`, and `@sentry/opentelemetry` bridge all landed with ADR-0024. The one honest nuance against "it's just an env var": `buildOtlpSpanProcessors(endpoint)` today constructs `new OTLPTraceExporter({ url: endpoint })` with no headers, and every SaaS backend requires an **auth header** (Grafana Cloud: Basic auth `instanceID:token`; Honeycomb: an `x-honeycomb-team` header). The OTLP exporter reads the standard `OTEL_EXPORTER_OTLP_HEADERS` env var when the constructor does not override it, so turn-on is *likely* configuration-only — but the turn-on ticket **verifies the header path** and, if needed, threads headers through the existing seam (a minimal extension, not a re-architecture). **Metrics adds dependencies** — `@opentelemetry/sdk-metrics` and an OTLP metrics exporter — named here so they are proposed-in-principle per CLAUDE.md's "propose a new top-level dependency first" rule, but **added only when the metrics ticket lands**, not now. Finally, **who may see traces** forward-references **ADR-0028 (sibling)**: trace and metric data live in the external SaaS account, governed by that account's own access control, and **for Phase 2 observability access stays with the operator (CEO)**; if office staff ever need observability access, that is governed by ADR-0028's role model, not granted ad hoc here.

## Alternatives considered

**Self-host the observability backend on the VPS (self-hosted SigNoz, or a self-hosted Grafana LGTM/Tempo stack).** Rejected on the ADR-0014 single-VPS resource constraint. An observability backend that stores traces and metrics needs its own datastore (ClickHouse for SigNoz; object storage + Tempo/Mimir for Grafana) and would compete for the same 2–4 GB of RAM as Postgres+PostGIS, Redis, the API, the web app, and Caddy — making the one-person ops box two systems to operate and undermining the very reliability budget (ADR-0011) it is meant to protect. The entire point of ADR-0024's env-gated *exporter* is to ship spans **out** to a hosted backend, not to stand one up on the same box. The self-hosted OTel **Collector** survives only in the narrower role of an on-box forwarding/scrubbing agent in front of a SaaS, which is lightweight enough to be acceptable.

**Datadog (the roadmap's third named candidate).** Rejected as the primary because its free tier does not include APM/distributed tracing — tracing is a paid product — so "Datadog free tier" cannot host the traces this upgrade exists to add without becoming a paid vendor relationship, and Datadog is the most expensive option at scale. It is a strong product; it is the wrong fit for a cost-sensitive single-company internal tool whose whole infra posture (ADR-0014) is "cheapest correct."

**Big-bang all three signals (logs + traces + metrics) at once.** Rejected in favour of the staged rollout (commitment 3). Traces are free to turn on (exporter exists); metrics need code and have nothing to measure until telematics ships; logs already work and are already trace-correlated. Landing all three together would couple the cheap, ready win (traces) to the not-yet-useful work (metrics with no telematics) and the lowest-value work (re-routing logs that already function), delaying the part that helps debugging today for no benefit.

**Ratio-sample everything from the start (e.g. 10%) to "be safe" on quota.** Rejected. At single-company volume, sampling throws away traces we can comfortably afford to keep and can hide the rare failure that is exactly what you reach for a trace to debug. 100% is both cheaper to reason about and more useful at this scale; the only real volume risk is the GPS-ping stream, which commitment 4 handles structurally (trace the batch job, not the ping) rather than by blanket sampling.

**Scrub sensitive span attributes with Sentry's `beforeSendSpan` hook alone.** Rejected as insufficient (commitment 5). `beforeSendSpan` scrubs only what Sentry sends to Sentry; the OTLP `BatchSpanProcessor` is a separate processor on the same TracerProvider and its egress would stay unscrubbed — precisely the path this upgrade adds. The scrub must sit in the OTLP egress path (a SpanProcessor/exporter wrapper); `beforeSendSpan` is at most a parallel addition for Sentry's own egress, not the mechanism.

**Defer the new SLIs until telematics is live and measured.** Rejected because ADR-0011 explicitly anticipated naming them in Phase 2, and choosing the indicators (what we will measure and roughly where the bar sits) is a design decision that should precede building the telematics path, not trail it — you instrument for the SLI you have already chosen. The targets are marked provisional precisely so that naming them now does not pretend to a precision the absent production data cannot yet support.

## Consequences

### What this makes easier

The day the PO picks a backend and provisions credentials, traces light up with a configuration change, not a code change — the ADR-0024 substrate pays off exactly as designed. Root-cause analysis across the Phase-2 distributed surface (driver app → API → BullMQ telematics workers → R2 uploads) becomes "follow the trace," and the existing SLI signals and the new telematics metrics become correlatable to the exact span tree that produced them. Picking a single managed vendor for all three signals keeps the one-person ops burden near zero (no observability infrastructure to run) and the cost near zero (free tier), both of which are load-bearing for a solo operator on a cost-sensitive internal tool. Naming the two new SLIs now gives the telematics and driver-app slices a measurement target to build toward rather than retrofit.

### What this makes harder

A new external vendor relationship enters the stack — an account, credentials (a new Tier 1 secret: the OTLP auth token, which lives only in the on-box env file per ADR-0014 and never in source or logs), a billing identity, and a data-residency consideration (trace/metric data leaving Nepal to the SaaS's region). The scrub seam (commitment 5) is now a permanent correctness obligation on every span shape: each new custom attribute added in Phase 2+ must be checked against the denylist, and the denylist must stay in sync with the pino `redact` list — drift between them is the failure mode, the same way Tailwind-vs-DESIGN.md drift is for the design system. Metrics instrumentation is genuine new surface (a MeterProvider, counter/histogram/gauge definitions) landing with the telematics slice.

### Costs we accept

- **These decisions are made without production telemetry (ADR-0025).** The backend choice, the 100% sampling policy, and especially the two provisional SLI targets are sized against assumptions, not measured Phase-1 load. We accept this and mark the targets provisional; the "Revisit when" below makes first-real-data a calibration trigger.
- **The OTLP export path is still not exercised against a real backend until the PO provisions one** — the same honest caveat ADR-0024 logged. This ADR decides the destination; it does not prove spans arrive. First real verification is the turn-on ticket against the chosen SaaS.
- **One vendor's free tier is a soft dependency.** A free tier can change its quotas or terms; the OTLP-native, env-gated design is the hedge — re-pointing `OTEL_EXPORTER_OTLP_ENDPOINT` (plus auth header) at a different OTLP backend is a configuration change, so the lock-in is shallow by construction.

## Revisit when

- **The first production deploy lands and real telemetry exists (ADR-0025).** Re-calibrate the two provisional SLI targets against measured ping-delivery and trip-start data, and re-examine whether 100% sampling and the chosen free tier still fit the real volume. This is the primary calibration trigger.
- **ADR-0027 sets the GPS tier.** The span-scrub denylist (commitment 5) for GPS coordinates is finalized against ADR-0027's tier decision; if ADR-0027 mandates tighter handling (e.g. a sensitive-location tier with truncation), the scrub seam and any future log-shipping inherit it.
- **The GPS-ping volume threatens the free-tier quota or the trace signal-to-noise.** Commitment 4's structural answer (trace the batch job, not the ping) is the first lever; ratio sampling on the ingestion path is the second; a paid tier or a different backend is the last.
- **Metrics outgrow what the chosen backend's free tier stores**, or a second SLI needs a histogram the metrics pipeline does not yet emit → revisit the metrics exporter and cardinality budget when the telematics slice's real metric shape is known.
- **A second human operator needs observability access** → revisit who-sees-traces against ADR-0028's role model rather than sharing the single operator account.
- **`@sentry/node` majors past v9 and changes the shared-sampler / `tracesSampler` surface** → the sampling decision in commitment 4 is expressed in terms of that surface; an SDK major re-confirms it, coordinated with ADR-0024's parallel "Revisit when" on the same dependency.
